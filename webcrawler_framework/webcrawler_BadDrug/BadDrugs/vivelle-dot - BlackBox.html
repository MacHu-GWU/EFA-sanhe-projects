Vivelle Dot Side Effects

  • Breast cancer
  • Uterine cancer
  • Stroke
  • Heart attack
  • Blood clots
  • Dementia
  • Gallbladder disease
  • Ovarian cancer
  • Breast lumps
  • Unusual vaginal bleeding
  • Dizziness and faintness
  • Changes in speech
  • Severe headaches
  • Chest pain
  • Shortness of breath
  • Pains in your legs
  • Changes in vision
  • Vomiting

Common Adverse Side Effects

Less severe and more common adverse side effects include:
  • Headache
  • Breast pain
  • Irregular vaginal bleeding or spotting
  • Stomach/abdominal cramps and bloating
  • Nausea and vomiting

Warnings & Recalls for Vivelle Dot

Contraindications

Some contraindications of Vivelle Dot | Estradiol Transdermal Patch include:
  • Abnormal vaginal bleeding
  • Known, suspected or a history of breast cancer
  • Known or suspected neoplasia which is dependent upon estrogen
  • Active deep vein thrombosis
  • Pulmonary embolism
  • Active and recent stroke or heart attack
  • Liver dysfunction or disease
  • Hypersensitivity to this drug
  • Known or suspected pregnancy

Who Should Not Take This Drug

Some warnings, preexisting conditions, and precautions that might hinder a patient’s ability to take Vivelle Dot | Estradiol Transdermal Patch include:
  • Women without hysterectomies who take progestins
  • High blood pressure
  • Hypertriglyceridemia
  • Impaired liver function
  • Cholestatic jaundice
  • Hypothyroidismy
  • Fluid retention
  • Hypocalcemia
  • Ovarian cancer
  • Endometriosis exacerbation

Vivelle Dot Treatment and Use

Vivelle Dot | Estradiol Transdermal Patch is a drug that is prescribed to menopausal women to alleviate symptoms of menopause attached to it such as night sweats, severe hot flashes, vaginal dryness and vaginal atrophy.  The drug company Novogyne Pharmaceuticals manufactures and markets Vivelle Dot | Estradiol Transdermal Patch.  Estradiol is a sex hormone associated mainly with females, however it is also present in males as a byproduct of testosterone levels.  As the name implies, this drug is administered in patch for an extended release effect.

The official FDA-labeled indications for this drug are for:

  • Abnormal vasomotor functionality as a result of menopause
  • Vaginal atrophy
  • Atrophic vulva
  • Metastatic breast cancer
  • Cancer of the prostate in males which is dependent upon androgen
  • Decreased estrogen levels
  • Menopause
  • Postmenopausal osteoporosis

Non-FDA labeled indications for this drug include:

  • Menstrual migraine
  • Mental distress
  • Postpartum depression
  • Urinary tract infection

How This Drug Works

Estrogens are known to regulate the transcription of a limited amount of genes.  They act by binding to the nuclear receptors of estrogen-responsive tissues of women.  Estrogens are also known to moderate the pituitary secretion of gonadotropins, follicle stimulating hormones and also the luteinizing hormones.  Estrogens do this through a negative feedback mechanism which reduces levels of these certain hormones evident in postmenopausal women.  Estradiol is the primary intracellular human estrogen and is exponentially more potent than its metabolites at the receptor level.

Dosages

Vivelle Dot | Estradiol Transdermal Patch has five dosages of ranging from 0.025mg, 0.0375mg, 0.05mg, 0.075mg, and 0.1 mg depending upon the necessity of the patient to have higher dosages or not.  The 0.025mg is exclusively for women who require Vivelle Dot | Estradiol Transdermal Patch for postmenopausal osteoporosis.
