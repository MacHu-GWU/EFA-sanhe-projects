Fabior Side Effects

Serious Side Effects of Fabior

  • Symptoms of a severe allergic reaction to Fabior include hives, having a difficult time breathing, and swelling of the lips, tongue, or throat.
  • Contact your doctor if you experience skin that burns, stings, or itches severely, severe redness, swelling or oozing.

Common Side Effects of Fabior

  • Fabior can cause an increase in photosensitivity. When using Fabior you should avoid direct natural sun light or sun lamps.
  • If your skin begins to peel, or turns red, wait until the irritation goes away before applying Fabior again.
  • You may experience mild irritation, dryness, and exfoliation on the skin where you apply Fabior.

Warnings & Recalls for Fabior

Fabior Warnings

  • If you are pregnant, do not use this medication. Fabior may cause harm to the fetus.
  • Fabior is flammable. Avoid open flames and smoking while taking Fabior.
  • Avoid using other products that can dry out your skin.
  • Tell your doctor if you have ever had a history of skin conditions, a family history of skin cancer, are sensitive to the sun, or work outdoors often.

Fabior Treatment and Use

Fabior | tazarotene is a foam retinoid used to treat moderate and severe acne in patients older than 12 years of age. Severe acne vulgaris is characterized by large, long lasting lesions, such as nodules and pus-filled cysts. Fabior is part of the acetylenic class of retinoids. Retinoids are derivatives of vitamin A that can be natural or synthesized.

How does Fabior Work?

Fabior improves the texture of the skin using a compound that acts similarly to vitamin A. This medication encourages the body to renew skin cells in affected areas more quickly than normal.

Fabior Dosage Information

Use a gentle soap to wash the affected skin and wait for it to dry completely before using Fabior.

Apply Fabior in a thin layer over the affected area once a day during the evening. Wash your hands after using Fabior. Use moisturizer after the medication has dried to keep your skin hydrated.

Keep the medication away from your eyes, mouth, and other mucous membranes, such as the nostrils, ears, genitals, and anus.
