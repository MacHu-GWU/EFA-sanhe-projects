Synthroid Side Effects

Synthroid | levothyroxine has been shown to cause side effects such as
  • nausea
  • vomiting
  • diarrhea
  • stomach cramps
  • weight loss
  • headache
  • fever
  • tremor
  • nervousness
  • irritability
  • excessive sweating
  • increase in appetite
  • changes in menstrual cycle
  • sensitivity to heat
  • temporary hair loss

Warnings & Recalls for Synthroid

Synthroid should not be used by patients who have suffered from conditions such as
  • heart attack
  • thyrotoxicosis
  • adrenal gland problems
There is no recall for this medication at this time.

Synthroid Treatment and Use

Synthroid | levothyroxineÂ is a thyroid hormone manufactured by Abbott Laboratories as an oral tablet. Synthroid replaces and increases thyroid hormone, which is vital for the maintenance of mental and physical function. Thyroid hormone is typically produced by the thyroid gland, but if the thyroid gland is damaged by radiation, medication, or removal, Synthroid provides a means of maintaining a healthy thyroid hormone level and reversing the negative side effects of hypothyroidism.

Synthroid | levothyroxine has been approved for the treatment of

  • Hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid)
  • Enlargement of the thyroid gland (goiter)
