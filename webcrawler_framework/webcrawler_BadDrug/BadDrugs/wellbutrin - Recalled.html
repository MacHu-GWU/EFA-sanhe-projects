Wellbutrin Side Effects

Wellbutrin side effects and adverse reactions include the following:
  • Anxiety, panic, or agitation
  • Hostility
  • Thrill-seeking behavior
  • Restlessness
  • Mood swings
  • Hallucinations
  • Heart palpitations
  • Insomnia
Other, less frequent side effects of Wellbutrin include:
  • Hair loss
  • Ulcers
  • Acne
  • Cold sores
  • Bronchitis
  • Memory loss
  • Confusion, and
  • Bladder infections

Warnings & Recalls for Wellbutrin

  • If you are an individual with a disorder affecting liver function, or if you exhibit symptoms of liver disease, you should limit dosage to 75 mg, twice daily.
  • Although Wellbutrin has in some rare instances lowered the seizure threshold, this medication has not been shown to cause weight gain or sexual dysfunction. In fact, weight loss and an increase in libido have been observed in those treated with Wellbutrin.
  • As is the case with all anti-depressant medications, Wellbutrin was given a black box warning due to reports of an increase in suicidal thinking among adolescents and young adults treated with the medication. The black box is the most stringent warning that the FDA can mandate for a drug. It is placed on the packaging insert and is separate from the usual side effects. In this instance, it indicates that Wellbutrin side effects could be serious or life threatening.
  • It is not recommended that patients with seizure disorder take Wellbutrin, as this drug is known to decrease the seizure threshold. If you have suffered with an eating disorder such as bulimia or anorexia nervosa, you should likewise not take this medication due to the increased risk of seizure. And if you have a known allergy to bupropion or any of the active or inactive ingredients in Wellbutrin, it is advised that you avoid taking it and pursue other treatment options.
  • It is important that you understand all of the risks associated with Wellbutrin before beginning this or any other anti-depressant medication regimen. If you have any questions about Wellbutrin and it’s adverse effects, it is important that you speak to your doctor or another healthcare provider immediately to prevent possible life-threatening complications.

Wellbutrin Treatment and Use

Wellbutrin | bupropion hydrochloride is an atypical, non tricyclic antidepressant that differs from other commonly prescribed mood-enhancing medications such as selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRI’s). Wellbutrin is manufactured by GlaxoSmithKline, though generic bupropion is made by several other drug makers.

Wellbutrin is a norepinephrine and dopamine reuptake inhibitor (NDRI). This pharmacological action is believed to relieve depression in those treated with Wellbutrin. Currently, bupropion containing drugs such as Wellbutrin, Zyban and Chantix (a smoking-cessation aid), are the only FDA approved NDRI’s.

How is Wellbutrin prescribed?

After your mental health professional or primary care physician (in some cases) decides that you are a candidate for Wellbutrin, he or she will make a dosing recommendation based on your individual medical history as well as your age, weight, and the extent to which your condition affects you.

Wellbutrin comes in oral tablet form and is taken two to four times daily, or in a once daily formula. Although many factors contribute to an individual’s physician-recommended dosage, the usual starting dose of Wellbutrin is 100mg, twice daily. Each individual dose of this medication should not exceed 150 mg (to avoid seizures), and maximum daily dose should never go over 450 mg in a 24 hour period. Doing this may raise your risk of seizure or other serious complications.
