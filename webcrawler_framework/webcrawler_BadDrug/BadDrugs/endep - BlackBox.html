Endep Side Effects

Severe Side Effects of Endep

  • You may develop a variety of cognitive or perceptive disorders while taking Endep. These may include slurred or unintelligible speech, dizziness, fainting, seizures, and hallucinations.
  • Tell your doctor if you notice bodily changes such as irregular heartbeats, muscle spasms, unexplained bleeding or bruising, yellowing of the skin or eyes, or intense discomfort in your legs, arms, or chest.
  • The onset of hives or swelling in the face or tongue may indicate a serious allergic reaction. Seek medical attention right away.

 Common Side Effects of Endep

  • Nausea, dry mouth, headaches, and drowsiness are common when taking Endep.
  • Digestive issues such as constipation, vomiting, and trouble passing urine are also common.
  • Users of Endep may experience changes in their sex drive or appetite.

Warnings & Recalls for Endep

Warnings About Endep

  • People who take antidepressants such as Endep may be at greater risk of attempting suicide than people who do not take antidepressants. The patient and his or her loved ones must be familiar with medicinal risks. Seek medical attention right away if the patient develops new or worsening depression, thoughts of self-harm or suicide, uncontrolled nervousness or anxiety, panic attacks, irregular sleeping patterns, and hostile or aggressive behaviors.
  • It is important for you to keep taking this medication even if you feel well. Consult your doctor before you discontinue Endep. Prematurely quitting Endep may result in undesired symptoms, such as nausea, fatigue, and headaches.
  • Store this medicine at room temperature in a dry location that is not exposed to direct heat or sunlight. As with all drugs and supplements, keep Endep out of the reach of children.

Endep Treatment and Use

Endep | Amitriptyline is a tricyclic antidepressant used to treat depression. Depression is a mental disorder in which patients experience overwhelming feelings of grief and hopelessness. Patients who take Endep have said their eating and sleeping habits are better, and they have more energy and lust for life.

How does Endep work?

Endep works to remedy chemical imbalances in the brain. People who suffer from depression are believed to have insufficient levels of neurotransmitters such as serotonin. Endep is prescribed to raise these levels.

Dosage

The Endep tablet is taken by mouth one to four times daily. Try to take Endep at the same time(s) every day. A certified physician will prescribe your ideal dosage. If you are instructed to ingest only one tablet per day, take it at bedtime to reduce the risk of daytime drowsiness.

Patients who take Endep usually start with a low dose and then gradually increase their dosage. It is important for you to keep taking this medication even if you feel well. Consult with your doctor before you discontinue Endep. Prematurely quitting Endep may result in undesired symptoms such as nausea, fatigue, and headaches.
