Estrasorb Side Effects

Common Side Effects:

  • Swollen Breasts
  • Vaginal Pain
  • Break-through bleeding
  • Acne or skin color changes
  • Migrane headaches

Serious Side Effects:

Warning: Estrasorb is a topical emulsion, and can cause premature puberty in a child who comes into contact with it.

  • Other serious side effects include:

  • Chest pain or heavy feeling

  • Jaundice

  • Abnormal vaginal bleeding

  • Sudden numbness or weakness

  • A lump in your breast

    Call your doctor if you are experiencing any of these side effects.

Warnings & Recalls for Estrasorb

WARNING: Estrasorb increases the risk of endometrial cancer. Monitor for abnormal vaginal bleeding. Estrogens with or without progestins should not be used for the prevention of cardiovascular disease. Increased risks of myocardial infarction, stroke, invasive breast cancer, pulmonary emboli, and deep vein thrombosis in postmenopausal women have been reported.

Estrasorb Treatment and Use

Estrasorb | Estradiol Topical is a topical gel or emulsion used in the treatment of hot flashes in menopausal women.

 

What it’s Used For:

Estrasorb contains an estrogen hormone called estradiol, which is used to regulate functions in the female body. Estrasorb treats symptoms caused by menopause, such as dryness in the vagina, burning in the vagina, and itching of the vagina.
