Warnings & Recalls for Unithroid (Levothyroxine)

Unithroid Black Box Warnings

Unithroid should not be used under any circumstances for the treatment of obesity or weight loss. Unithroid should not be considered as an effective treatment for these conditions, and large doses may cause serious, potentially fatal, side effects, such as lethal overdose from toxic levels of thyroid hormone in the body.

Other Warnings Associated with Unithroid

Continued usage of Unithroid is known to cause the long term side effect of osteoporosis, which is the decreasing of bone density. Just like any other medication, Unithroid carries the risk of potential allergic reaction. Immediately notify a healthcare professional if you experience rash, itching or swelling, particularly in the face or throat, severe dizziness, or have trouble breathing.Patients who suffer from allergies related to any of the components contained in Unithroid or are allergic to pork should consult their doctor before taking this medication.Patients who have a history of the following conditions should first consult a doctor before using Unithroid:
  • diabetes
  • osteoporosis
  • atherosclerosis
  • cardiovascular events, such as high blood pressure, chest pain, or heart attack
Patients who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or wanting to come pregnant should consult with a doctor before taking or continuing the use of Unithroid.

Unithroid (Levothyroxine) Treatment and Use

Unithroid (Levothyroxine) is an artificial thyroid hormone manufactured by Jerome Stevens Pharmaceuticals which is prescribed to treat hypothyroidism, a condition where the thyroid is underactive and unable to sustain its own hormone levels. This hormone, known as thyroxine, regulates energy and metabolism, and effects many bodily functions. Unithroid acts as a substitute for the thyroxine that an underactive thyroid is unable to produce, and helps maintain both mental and physical activities that the thyroid is essential to regulating.

Symptoms of an Underactive Thyroid

  • Results in poor growth in children
  • Slowed speech
  • Lack of energy or listlessness
  • Sudden weight gain
  • Hair loss
  • Dried out or thickened skin
  • Increased sensitivity to cold

Unithroid Dosage Information

Unithroid is an oral tablet which is typically prescribed in small doses before being increased as your healthcare physician sees fit. Unithroid can also be used in the treatment and prevention of goiter, which is an enlargement of the thyroid gland brought on by hormone imbalances and radiation treatments resulting from cancer.
