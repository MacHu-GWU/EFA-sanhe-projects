Keppra Side Effects

Keppra | Levetiracetam adverse events include:
  • Skin problems
  • Hallucinations
  • Problems with gait
  • General malaise
  • Fatigue
  • Dizziness
  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Increased thoughts of suicide
  • Congestion
If you have experienced any of these Keppra | Levetiracetam side effects, contact your doctor. They could be indicative of more severe problems. For any symptoms or side effects that have not been listed here, or for previous severe reactions that you have had to Keppra, fill out our report a bad drug form.

Warnings & Recalls for Keppra

  • In 2009, the FDA released a revised black box warning for Keppra due to increased suicidal ideation in users of the drug.
  • For the injectable version of Keppra for epilepsy, the FDA warns of increased behavioral abnormalities associated with persons being treated for general tonic-clonic seizures in this safety information page.
  • Inform your physician of any pre-existing kidney or liver disease that you may have before taking Keppra.
  • Keppra has been given a Pregnancy Class C rating in the Pregnancy Category system, which means that though the harm to pregnant women and fetuses has not been outright studied, a correlation has been observed. If you are pregnant or considering becoming pregnant, speak to your doctor before taking Keppra and enroll in the North American Antiepileptic Drug (NAAED) Pregnancy Registry.

Keppra Treatment and Use

Keppra | Levetiracetam is an anticonvulsant medication made by UBC Pharmaceuticals used to treat certain types of seizures caused by epilepsy in combination with other drugs like Topamax along with certain lifestyle changes.

Though it is not known exactly how Keppra counters seizures, it is believed to reduce the changes in electrical activity in the brain that causes them.

How to take Keppra

Keppra | Levetiracetam for epilepsy comes in pill, oral solution, and IV form and should be taken twice daily, at the same times each day to ensure that a steady level of the drug remains in your system.

You may take Keppra alone or with food. If you begin to experience adverse reactions related to the stomach while taking Keppra, take this medication with food.

Your doctor will take into consideration your age, medical history, and various other factors to determine whether Keppra for epilepsy is the right drug for you. If you have any questions or concerns about Keppra  or Keppra side effects please speak to your healthcare provider at once.
