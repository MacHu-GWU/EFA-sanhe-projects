AndroGel 1% Side Effects

Serious Side Effects:

  • Potential increase in risk of prostate cancer
  • Enlargement of prostate
  • Reduction of sperm count
  • Blood clots in legs
  • Enlarged and painful male breasts

Common Side Effects:

  • Skin irritation
  • High cholesterol levels
  • Increase in red blood cell count
  • Acne
Talk to your doctor about the side effects of using AndroGel 1%.

Warnings & Recalls for AndroGel 1%

Family Planning

  • AndroGel should not be used on women, especially those who are pregnant, may become pregnant or are breastfeeding. These women should avoid contact with the area where AndroGel has been applied.
  • AndroGel has been listed in Pregnancy Category X, and therefore may harm an unborn or breastfeeding baby.

Warnings

  • The effects of AndroGel can be transferred from one body to another through physical contact, therefore women or children should avoid contact and should be washed well if they should come into contact with the affected area.
  • AndroGel should be discontinued if signs of puberty are seen in a child or testosterone induced changes like body hair or increased acne in women that may have occurred through accidental contact.
  • AndroGel should dry completely before going near an open flame as it is flammable until dry.
  • Patients who have had breast cancer or prostate cancer, urinary problems, heart, liver or kidney complications or sleep apnea should consult with a doctor before starting this medication.

Drug Interactions

Insulin, and corticosteroids could conflict with AndroGel and vice versa. Patients taking any of these medications should consult with a physician before combining these drugs.

AndroGel 1% Treatment and Use

AndroGel 1% is topical gel, used in the daily treatment of testosterone replacement therapy. It is specifically designed to bring T levels to a normal level, and maintain them at that peak. With this replacement, symptoms should subside or improve. It is used to treat males who have little to no testosterone.

How it Works

AndroGel 1% contains testosterone, and is absorbed into your skin, helping to bring T levels back to normal. It does not cure low levels of testosterone, but instead is used to daily to treat symptoms.

Androgel 1% may help to correct erectile dysfunction and increases libido.

Dosage

Ask your doctor what dosage is right for you but typically is applied to the shoulders, upper arms, and stomach area. Those using AndroGel are advised to NOT use it on the following areas: penis, scrotum, chest or back.

For patients to lower the chances of AndroGel being accidentally transferred, they should apply it to an area that will be covered with clothing once the area is dry. After applying the gel, hands should be completely washed immediately.
