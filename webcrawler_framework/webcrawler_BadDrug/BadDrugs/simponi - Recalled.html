Simponi Side Effects

Severe Side Effects of Simponi

  • Hives, shortness of breath and swelling in the face, mouth, lips, tongue, eyes or throat may all be signs of a severe allergic reaction to Simponi.
  • Patients who take Simponi may develop life-threatening conditions such as sepsis, permanent liver damage, cancers and viral, fungal or bacterial infections.
  • Tell your doctor right away if you have new or worsening psoriasis, congestive heart failure or hepatitis B symptoms.
  • Other serious side effects include blurred vision, blisters, intense stomach or joint pain and weakness or tingling in the arms and legs.

Common Side Effects of Simponi

  • Seek medical attention if redness or swelling at the injection site worsens or refuses to go away.

Warnings & Recalls for Simponi

Black Box Warning for Simponi

  • Tell your doctor about any medical conditions that you may have, especially if you have hepatitis, congestive heart failure, lupus, diabetes, cancer, human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) or multiple sclerosis (MS).
  • Simponi may lower your ability to fight infections and may increase your risk of having serious viral, fungal and bacterial infections. You should not start Simponi if you are receiving treatment for an infection or are exhibiting symptoms of an infection such as high fever, sweating, muscle aches, coughing, bloody phlegm or weight loss.
  • Before you start Simponi, your doctor will screen you for tuberculosis (TB). He or she will monitor you for signs of TB during your treatment. Even so, it is important to let your doctor know if you have been in close contact with someone who has TB.
  • The hepatitis B virus may already be inside you, and you don't even know it. Call your doctor right away if you have any of these symptoms of hepatitis B: intense fatigue, yellowing of the skin or eyes, poor appetite, nausea, vomiting, muscle aches, high fever, dark urine, chills, skin rash or stomach pain.
  • Children and adolescents who have received treatments similar to Simponi have developed cancers such as lymphoma. Some of these cases have been fatal. Simponi, therefore, is rarely prescribed to kids, but your doctor may decide that it is the best option for treatment. Before your child starts Simponi, learn about its medicinal risks and benefits and tell your doctor about any major weight loss, unexplained bruising or bleeding and swelling glands in the neck, underarms and groin.
  • Alert your doctor if you have lived in either the Ohio or Mississippi River valleys, where exposure to certainÂ  fungal infections are common.
  • You may have new or worsening congestive heart failure while on Simponi. Seek medical attention if you develop symptoms of heart failure such as shortness of breath or swelling in the lower legs or feet.
  • Rarely, patients who undergo treatments that are similar to Simponi have developed nervous system disorders such as multiple sclerosis (MS). Contact your physician right away if you have symptoms like blurry vision, weakness in the arms and legs or numbness in any part of your body.
  • Severe liver problems can also happen if you are on Simponi. Seek medical attention if you experience intense fatigue, poor appetite, vomiting, yellowing in the skin or eyes, or extreme stomach pain.
  • Lupus-like symptoms may occur while you receive Simponi treatment. Tell your doctor if you develop a rash, sensitivity to the sun, joint or muscle pain, frequent fatigue, chest pain, breathing problems or swelling in the feet, ankles or legs.

General Warnings About Simponi

  • Talk to your doctor if you are on Kineret | anakinra, Orencia | abatacept, Rituxan | rituximab or Tysabri | natalizumab. Your risk of infection may be higher if you are on any of these medications.
  • Avoid other TNF inhibitors such as Remicade | infliximab, Humira | adalimumab, Enbrel | etanercept and Simponi | golimumab.
  • The needle cover for the Simponi autoinjector and syringe contains dry natural rubber. Tell your doctor if you are alergic to latex or rubber.
  • Tell your doctor if you are pregnant or become pregnant while receiving Simponi treatment. Your baby may need to delay its necessary vaccinations.

Simponi Treatment and Use

Simponi | Golimumab is a prescribed tumor necrosis factor (TNF) inhibitor that is approved for the treatment of moderate to severe rheumatoid arthritis (RA), psoriatic arthritis and ankylosing spondylitis (a chronic inflammatory disease that causes joint pain in the spinal cord and pelvis). Simponi is developed by Janssen Biotech, a division of Johnson & Johnson.

How does Simponi work?

Simponi works by restricting the activities of tumor necrosis factors, which are molecules in the human body that cause inflammation. Rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis and ankylosing spondylitis are all autoimmune disorders, conditions in which the body’s immune system attacks healthy parts of itself, thus resulting in pain, swelling and damage. By blocking the effects of TNF, Simponi relieves pain and inflammation in patients.

Dosage

Simponi is available in a SmartJect autoinjector or pre-filled syringe. The recommended dosage is one 50 milligram injection per month.

You will receive your first dose of Simponi in your doctor’s office. After that, you may use Simponi at home as long as your doctor shows you or a friend the proper way of injecting it.

Store Simponi in your refrigerator until 30 minutes before you are ready to use it. Do not freeze it. Remove the autoinjector or syringe from its carton and place it on a flat surface so that it may warm up to room temperature. Do not attempt to warm it in boiling water or in the microwave. Leave the cap of the autoinjector or syringe on while you are warming it. The cap should be removed no more than five minutes before you administer Simponi.

Look through the viewing window of the syringe or autoinjector and make sure that the clear or pale yellow liquid in it is not cloudy or discolored. If it contains large, colored particles, do not use it. Instead, contact your pharmacist and report the problem. Additionally, ask about the best way of discarding used syringes or autoinjectors.

Simponi may be injected anywhere on the thighs or stomach, except the navel and the two-inch area around it. Do not inject Simponi into tender, bruised, red, scarred or stretch-marked skin. Be sure to rotate application sites every time. Choose a new spot that is at least one inch away from a previously used spot. If a friend or relative is giving you the dosage, he or she may also inject Simponi into your upper arms.

It is important to continue using Simponi even if you feel well. Speak with your doctor before you decide to discontinue your injections.

Patients who are on Simponi should not receive vaccinations.
