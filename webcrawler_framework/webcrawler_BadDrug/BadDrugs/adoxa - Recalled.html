Adoxa Side Effects

Severe side effects of Adoxa

  • Allergic reactions include difficulty breathing or swallowing and facial swelling of the eyes, lips, or tongue.
  • Severe Headaches.
  • Yellowing of the Skin or eyes.
  • Blurred Vision.
  • Adoxa may cause severe diarrhea (Clostridium difficile) which is associated with the eradication of beneficial gut flora.
  • Severe yeast infections may occur during prolonged use of Adoxa or any antibiotic.

Common side effects of Adoxa

  • Gastrointestinal Side Effects such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.
  • Increased susceptibility to sunburns.
  • Vaginal and/or rectal itching.
  • Mouth Sores.

Warnings & Recalls for Adoxa

Why was Adoxa recalled?

  • On September 16th, 2011, Ben Venue Laboratories, Inc. recalled Doxycycline Hyclate for Injection 100 mg per 15 ml vial due to faulty stoppers and violation of current good manufacturing practices.  The National Drug Code is 55390-110-10 and the recall number is D-1001-2011.  The expiration date on the recalled lots are 9/30/2011 and 10/31/2011.  For label lot numbers and manufacturing lot numbers, please check the FDA Enforcement Report for September 28, 2011.

Warnings

  • Adoxa is a Pregnancy Category class D drug and may harm an unborn baby.  Permanent tooth discoloration and altered bone development may occur in the baby if the mother has taken Adoxa during pregnancy.  The medication may also pass through breast milk and should be avoided during breastfeeding.
  • Adoxa may lower effectiveness of birth control pills.
  • Avoid excessive exposure to sunlight while taking Adoxa.
  • Adoxa is not appropriate for children younger than 8 (permanent discoloration of teeth may occur).
  • Antibiotic-resistant infections may occur from skipped doses or stopping the medication prematurely.
  • There is a potential for allergic reaction for those who react to tetracycline antibiotics and/or sulfites.
  • Avoid taking iron supplements, multivitamins, calcium supplements, antacids, and laxatives for 2 hours before and after taking Adoxa.
 

Adoxa Treatment and Use

Adoxa| doxycycline is a tetracycline antibiotic used to treat a number of bacterial infections including skin infections, acne or rosacea, respiratory tract infections, urinary tract infections, lyme disease, and inhaled anthrax.  It is also used to treat malaria.  Adoxa was  marketed as an acne medication by Doak Dermatologics, but doxycycline is a commonly prescribed antibiotic and is widely manufactured.

How does Adoxa work?

Adoxa interferes with the growth and inhibits the spread of bacteria in the body by preventing the creation of protein that the bacteria needs to survive.

Dosage

Adoxa comes in 50 mg, 75 mg, 100 mg, and 150 mg tablet, capsule, and injection forms.  The initial dosage of Adoxa is 200 mg divided into two doses for the first day.  Afterwards, the daily dose is lowered to 100 mg once a day or 50 mg twice a day.  Adoxa should be taken on an empty stomach.
