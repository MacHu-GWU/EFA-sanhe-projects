Aviane Side Effects

Serious Side Effects of Aviane

  • Stop taking Aviane and get medical attention of you feel weakness or experience a sudden numbness on one side of your body.
  • If you suddenly develop an acute headache, become confused, see changes in your vision, have trouble speaking, or lose control of your balance, talk to your doctor immediately.
  • Talk to your doctor as soon as possible if you develop a pain or heavy feeling in your chest, a pain that radiates to your arm or shoulders.
  • Seek medical help if you start to cough, cough up blood, wheeze, or breathe rapidly.
  • If your hands, ankles, or feet start to swell, stop taking Aviane and talk to your doctor.
  • Aviane may cause symptoms of depression. These symptoms include trouble sleeping, general weakness or tiredness, and changes in your mood.
  • Self examine yourself for any lumps that may develop in your breast. If you find one, talk to your doctor immediately.

Common Side Effects of Aviane

  • Aviane may cause your breasts to swell or feel tender, change your menstrual periods, cause vaginal itching or discharge, and decrease your sex drive.
  • When you first begin taking Aviane, you may experience mild nausea. It may also cause you to develop stomach cramps, bloating, or vomit.
  • You might observe a change in your weight or appetite after you start taking Aviane.
  • After taking Aviane you may experience increased growth of your hair, loss of the hair on your scalp, darkening of the skin on your face, or develop freckles.

Warnings & Recalls for Aviane

Aviane Black Box Warning

  • Avoid smoking while taking Aviane. It may increase your risk of developing serious cardiovascular complications. Women over the age of 35 are especially at risk.

Aviane Warning

  • You should not take Aviane if you are currently pregnant or have recently had a baby. It may cause birth defects. If you suspect you have become pregnant while taking Aviane, contact your doctor immediately.
  • If you are breast feeding, you should not take Aviane. It can slow your ability to produce milk and may harm a breast feeding baby.
  • Discuss all medications that you are taking or are planning on taking before you begin taking Aviane. Other medications may reduce Aviane's effectiveness. This includes antibotics, HIV or AIDS medicine, drugs to treat seizures, some sedatives, and hepatitis C medicines.
  • If you have or have had  heart disease, issues with blood circulation or clotting, a disease of the liver, unusual vaginal bleeding, or jaundice, you should not take Aviane.
  • If you have high blood pressure, increased levels of triglycerides, high cholesterol, have had depression, a thyroid condition, diabetes, experience seizures, tuberculosis, or any history of disease or a lump in a breast, talk to your doctor to see if Aviane is safe for you to take.

Aviane Treatment and Use

Aviane | Levonorgestrel and Ethinyl Estradiol is a combination of hormones used as a contraceptive to prevent pregnancy. It will not prevent the transfer of sexually transmitted diseases, such as HIV and AIDS. You will need a condom to reduce your risk of contracting an STD. When taken properly, birth control pills have a  relatively low rate of failure (less than 1% a year).

How does Aviane work?

Aviane prevents pregnancy in a number of ways. Primarily, it prevents ovulation, the release of an egg from a woman’s ovaries. It also alters cervical mucus, making it harder for sperm to enter the uterus. I can also change the lining of the uterus, making it difficult for a for a fertilized egg achieve implantation.

Aviane Dosage Information

Aviane is to be taken once a daily for three weeks at a time followed by one week without a dose. This week gives your body time to develop a regular menstrual cycle.

Forgetting a pill can drastically increase your chances of becoming pregnant. If you forget to take a pill one day, you may take two pills the following day. If you happen to forget to take Aviane for two days in a row, take two pills for the next two days, then return to one a day.

You may need to use a secondary form of birth control when you first start taking Aviane to prevent pregnancy.
