Lantus Side Effects

Lantus | insulin glargine [rDNA origin] injection side effects and adverse reactions include:
  • Allergic reaction
  • Hypoglycemia
  • Discomfort at the injection site

Warnings & Recalls for Lantus

  • Do not use this medication if you are allergic to any of the elements that make it up. Tell your doctor about any food or drug allergies that you may have.
  • Tell your doctor about any liver or kidney disease you may have before using Lantus.
  • Tell your doctor about any medications that you may be taking as some of these may interact adversely with Lantus.
  • The FDA released a safety alert in 2011 that warned of a higher risk of cancer in those treated with Lantus. It is important that you know this risk before using this medication.

Lantus Treatment and Use

Lantus | insulin glargine [rDNA origin] injection is a once-daily, long lasting, prefilled injection pen made by sanofi-aventis U.S. LLC. that is indicated for the treatment of type 1 diabetes in adults and children and type 2 diabetes in adults.

This medication is not recommended for the treatment of diabetic ketoacidosis. If you are suffering from this condition, intravenous short-acting insulin is indicated.

How Lantus Long-Acting Insulin works

This treatment gives the diabetes sufferer level amount of insulin in the body. This mimics the normal pancreatic secretion of insulin.

Lantus administration

The Lantus insulin injection pen has a pre-filled syringe that should be injected into the body at the same time daily.

When administering, keep the injection button pressed all the way down and count to 10 to ensure that all of the drug has been injected into the patient.

An instruction leaflet comes with the injection pen. To ensure that the patient gets the recommended dose of insulin, that individual or their caregiver should familiarize themselves with the directions on the leaflet.
