Warnings & Recalls for Buspar

Before beginning treatment with Buspar | buspiranone tell your doctor about the following:
  • liver disease
  • kidney disease
  • respiratory illness
  • if you are pregnant, nursing, think you may be pregnant, or are considering becoming pregnant
  • history of psychotic episodes

Buspar Treatment and Use

Buspar | buspirone is an anti-anxiety medication indicated for short term relief of symptoms of panic and generalized anxiety disorder such as:

  • fear
  • tension
  • rapid thoughts
  • sweating
  • fast heartbeat
  • irritability

Buspar | buspiranone is also used to treat obsessive compulsive disorder, social phobia, and off label to supplement SSRI therapy for depression with drugs like sertraline, Paxil, and Viibryd.

How does Buspar | buspiranone work?

Buspar | buspiranone is unrelated to any of the other drugs commonly used to treat anxiety. It is believed that this medication acts on both the serotonin and dopamine receptors in the brain, inhibiting too much of these chemicals to be reabsorbed. This is thought to alleviate the symptoms of panic.

How to take Buspar?

  • Use this medication exactly as prescribed by your doctor.
  • Buspar comes in tablet form and is taken orally one to three times daily.
  • You should take this drug consistently either with food or without food.
  • If you have any questions about Buspar | buspiranone or how to take this medication, speak to your doctor.
