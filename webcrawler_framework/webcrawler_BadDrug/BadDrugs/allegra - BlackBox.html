Allegra Side Effects

Serious Adverse Side Effects

Some side effects of Allegra | Fexofenadine can be quite serious, including:
  • hives
  • rash
  • itching
  • difficulty breathing or swallowing
  • swelling of the face, throat, tongue, lips, eyes, hands, feet, ankles, or lower legs
  • hoarseness
  • difficulty swallowing or breathing

More Common Side Effects

More common and less severe side effects of this drug are:
  • headache
  • dizziness
  • diarrhea
  • vomiting
  • pain in the arms, legs, or back
  • pain
  • pain during menstrual period
  • cough

A contraindication of Allegra | Fexofenadine is hypersensitivity to its active component “fexofenadine”.Some warnings and precautions before taking Allegra | Fexofenadine are:
  • concurrent administration of aluminum or magnesium containing antacid within 15 minutes of taking this drug

Allegra Treatment and Use

Allegra | Fexofenadine is an antihistamine pharmaceutical drug that’s used for the treatment of hay fever, hives and allergy symptoms.  The non-FDA indications for this drug are for pretreatment of hymenoptera immunotherapy and perennial allergic rhinitis.  It had been originally developed as a successor drug to terfenadine, which is an antihistamine drug that had serious adverse side effects such as QT-interval prolongation, which can cause potentially lethal cardiac arrhythmia.

Allegra | Fexofenadine, like other 2nd and 3rd generation antihistamines, doesn’t cross the blood-brain barrier, thus it doesn’t lead to adverse side effects such as drowsiness.  Allegra | Fexofenadine works by being an antagonist to the H1receptor.

Allegra | Fexofenadine comes either as a tablet or a suspension liquid to take orally.  This drug is meant to be taken with water at least once or twice a day.

Since its introduction into the market and approval by the FDA in 1996, Allegra | Fexofenadine has garnered about $688 million in sales for the drug company Sanofi-Aventis in the year 2008.

Mechanism of Action

Allegra | Fexofenadine inhibits the release of histamine from mast cells and selectively antagonizes peripheral H1receptor activity.
