Forteo Side Effects

Forteo | teriparatide injection has been shown to cause side effects such as
  • dizziness
  • pain, redness, bruising, itching, or swelling at the point of injection
  • leg cramps
  • joint pain
  • cold-like symptoms, such as cough, sore throat, or runny nose
  • headache
  • neck pain
  • nausea
  • constipation
  • diarrhea

Warnings & Recalls for Forteo

  • Patients who smoke or consume alcohol have a higher risk of fractures and should avoid smoking or drinking while taking Forteo. These habits can decrease bone mineral density, and weaken the strength of your bones.
  • Those who suffer from bone disorders such as Paget's disease, high levels of calcium in the blood, hyperparathyroidism, a history of bone cancer, or kideney stones should inform their doctor before taking Faceo, as your dosage may need to be adjusted and you may need to undergo testing.
  • Forteo has received a pregnancy Category C and should not be used by those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant.
  • Animal studies have shown that Forteo can cause or increase the risk for a condition known as osteosarcoma. For this reason, Forteo should not be taken to prevent osteoporosis, or unless your have already suffered from one break or have not responded to any other medications.

Forteo Treatment and Use

Forteo | teriparatide injection is a synthetic version of a hormone naturally produced in the body known as parathyroid hormone (PTH). Forteo is manufactured by Eli Lilly as a solution which is injected under the skin in the thigh or lower stomach area. Forteo works to strengthen bones by increasing their thickness, or density, and preventing the occurrence of fractures.

Forteo | teriparatide injection has been approved for the treatment of osteoporosis in patients who

  • Have experienced menopause
  • Are taking corticosteroids, which have been shown to thin bones in some patients
