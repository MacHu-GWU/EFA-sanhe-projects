Natazia Side Effects

Serious Side Effects

Call your doctor immediately, and stop using Natazia if any of these serious side effects occur
  • Numbness or weakness on one side of body
  • Chest pain and heavy feelings, pain that spreads through the shoulders, and causes nausea and sweating
  • Coughing up blood
  • Swelling of the hands, ankles, or feet
  • Breast lumps
  • Depression
 

Common Side Effects

  • Vaginal itching or discharge
  • Decreased libido
  • Freckles
  • Weight gain
 

Warnings & Recalls for Natazia

No reported recalls for Natazia

Natazia Treatment and Use

 

Natazia is a contraceptive for women, used to prevent ovulation. When used, Natazia enlists the help of female hormones to stop the user from ovulating, but it also makes it more difficult for sperm to enter the uterus or to fertilize an egg. It is used to prevent pregnancy.

Natazia is also given to treat excessive or heavy menstruation.

 

How it Works

Natazia contains two active ingredients: dienogest and estradiol. These are female hormones, and they work to stop the body from releasing an egg from the ovaries. Natazia also changes the mucus and lining of the cervical area, to impede sperm from traveling an egg and fertilizing it.

 

Dosing

Natazia is a pill, taken orally, once a day. It is important that the user take the pills in the exact order that they are prescribed. It is also advised that other forms of birth control, such as condoms, be used in conjunction with Natazia for maximum results.
