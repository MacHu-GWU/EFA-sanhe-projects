Protopic Side Effects

Protopic | tacrolimus side effects and adverse reactions include:
  • Severe allergic reaction
  • Back pain
  • Increased skin sensitivity
  • Unusual growths on the skin
  • Skin infection
  • Folliculitis
  • Rash
  • Tingling
  • Dermatitis
  • Nausea
  • Weakness

Warnings & Recalls for Protopic

  • Tell your doctor if you have Netherton's Syndrome before beginning treatment with Protopic ointment.
  • Do not use Protopic ointment for extended periods of time
  • In 2005 the FDA released a public health advisory to warn of the possible link between Protopic and cancer and in 2006 Protopic was given a black box warning.
  • Avoid excessive ultraviolet exposure while being treated with Protopic ointment.
  • Tell your doctor if you are pregnant, think you are pregnant, or are considering becoming pregnant before using Protopic ointment.

Protopic Treatment and Use

Protopic | tacrolimus is a topical calcineurin inhibitor marketed by Astrellas and used to treat eczema and related forms of dermatitis.

Protopic comes in ointment form and is available in a 0.1% (for adults and children 15 years and older) and 0.03% (for children 2-15 years old) strengths. The smallest amount possible is to be used on the eczema affected skin areas.

Do not bathe or swim shortly after applying Protopic | tacrolimus ointment as this may wash off the medication.
