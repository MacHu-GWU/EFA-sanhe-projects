Restasis Side Effects

Serious Side Effects of Restasis

  • If your side effects become persistent or become progressively worse, stop using Restasis and contact your doctor.
  • A serious allergic reaction to Restasis is not likely. However, the symptoms of an allergic reaction to Restasis include sever itchiness or rashes, swelling of the face, mouth, or throat, becoming dizzy, and having a hard time breathing.
  • If you experience any unusual side effects while taking Restasis, talk to your doctor.

Common Side Effects of Restasis

  • You may experience discharge of the eye or an abundance of tears after using Restasis.
  • Restasis can cause redness of the eye, blurred vision, and changes in vision. Do not do anything that requires your full visual potential, such as driving, until you know how Restasis will affect your vision.
  • After using Restasis, tour eyes may burn, itch, sting, or become painful. You may also feel as though there is a foreign object in your eye.

Warnings & Recalls for Restasis

Restasis Warnings

  • Let your doctor know about any other eye drops you are using before taking Restasis.
  • If you will be using Restasis while using artificial tears, wait 15 minutes before or after applying Restasis.
  • Do not apply Restasis while you are wearing contact lenses. Remove contacts before using Restasis and wait 15 minutes before you put them back in.
  • This medication does not help tear production in patents that are that use other anti-inflammatory eye drops or patients who currently use tear duct plugs.
  • If you have an eye infection, you should not use Restasis.
  • Restasis has not been tested for use in patients that have had a herpes infection of the eye.

Restasis Treatment and Use

Restasis | Cyclosporine Ophthalmic Emulsion is a medication used to treat Chronic Dry Eye by increasing the eye’s ability to create tears. It is the first prescription ocular emulsion that threats the reduced ability of tear production that may be caused by keratoconjunctivitis sicca. When Chronic Dry Eye goes untreated, it may lead to eye damage and impaired vision.

How does Restasis work?

Examination of how exactly Restasis works is still underway. It is believed to encourage the immune system to adjust inflammation in the eye and help normal tear creation by affecting the surface of the eye and lacrimal gland. The Cyclosporine in Restasis is an immunosuppressive that may aid reduction of eye inflammation.

Restasis Dosage information

Restasis comes as a liquid in  small single use containers. The emulsion should be dropped into the eyes two times daily, 12 hours apart.

Wash your hands with soap and water before applying Restasis. Turn the vial over until the liquid is no longer see-though. Once the vial is opened, do not touch the dropper tip or let it touch anything else. To properly apply Restasis, pull down your lower eyelid and apply a single drop into the pocket that is formed. After application, close your eye for a few minutes while  facing the floor without blinking or squeezing your eyes. If your doctor prescribes more than one drop per eye, wait five minutes before the next application.

Because the containers are meant to be single use, throw away the container, even if there is remaining product.
