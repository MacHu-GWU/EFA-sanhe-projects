Medrol Side Effects

Serious Side Effects of Medrol

  • Contact your doctor immediately if you experience a skin rash, swelling of the face or lower legs, seizures, problems with your vision, excessive weakness in the muscles, a cold that lasts longer than usual, coughing up blood, or black/tarry stool.
  • Report any unusual weight gain to your doctor.
  • Extended use of a corticosteroid can produce cataracts, glaucoma with a possibility of never damage, and contribute to eye infections.
  • If you have any signs of an infection, talk to your doctor. Symptoms can include extended fever, cough or sore throat, pain when urinating, and pain or discharge of the ye.

Common Side Effects of Medrol

  • Medrol may cause your stomach to be come upset, other stomach irritations, and vomiting.
  • While taking Medrol you may experience headaches, dizziness, trouble sleeping, restlessness, depression, mood swings and anxiety.
  • Medrol can cause outbreaks of acne, an increase in hair growth, and make you susceptible to bruising.
  • Menstrual periods can become irregular when taking Medrol.
  • Diabetic patients may experience an increase in blood sugar levels. Adjust your diabetes medication or diet to compensate.

Warnings & Recalls for Medrol

Medrol Warnings

  • Talk to your doctor before taking Medrol if you have a fungal infection.
  • Make sure your doctor is aware if you have a history of liver disease, kidney disease, heart disease, intestinal complications, blood disorders, an underactive thyroid, mental illness, osteoporosis, herpes of the eye, tuberculosis, seizures, or high blood pressure.
  • While taking Medrol, your doctor might ask you to have a diet that includes less sodium or salt and is high in potassium or protein.
  • Do not take any vaccinations, immunizations, or skin tests while taking Medrol without consulting your doctor.
  • Medrol can stunt bone growth in children.
  • Taking Medrol can inhibit the body's ability to respond to physical stress. Tell your doctor before getting any emergency treatments, including dental work.
  • Avoid alcohol while taking Medrol. It can increase your chances of stomach bleeding and intestinal damage.
  • Grapefruit can enhance side effects of Medrol. Avoid eating it while taking Medrol .

Medrol Drug Interactions

  • Let your doctor know if you are taking blood thinners, other arthritis medications, a diuretric, estrogen, an oral contraceptie, or vitamins.
  • Medrol can enhance or decrease the effects of an anticoagulant.
  • Use aspirin with caution if you have hypoprothrombinemia.
 

Medrol Treatment and Use

Medrol | Methylprednisolone Oral is a corticosteroid used to reduce inflammation. It is used as a treatment for several complications including skin, kidney, thyroid, eye, intestinal, and eye disorders. It can also be used to treat certain types of arthritis, acute allergies, and asthma. In some cases, it is used as a treatment or cancer.

How does Medrol work?

The methylprednisole in Medrol mimics a hormone that adrenal glands produce. Medrol helps replace this hormone when the body is unable to make enough. When used in medications, it is used for its ability to reduce inflammation in the body’s organ systems.

Medrol Dosage Information

Doses of Medrol can range from 4mg to 48mg a day, variant on the patient and their symptoms. Patients should only take the minimum effective dose for their complications based on the prescription instructions of their physician.

Medrol Dose Pack – Dosage

Methlyprednisolone comes in a package designed to give six days of Medrol in steadily decreasing amounts. Day 1 provides 6 – 4mg. pills, Day 2 provides 5, Day 3 provides 4, etc. So that over six days a patient takes 24mg on day one and only 4 mg. on the final day.

Medrol should be taken with food or milk to decrease the effects of an upset stomach. Medrol commonly causes upset stomach if not taken with food.

If Medrol is to be taken for an extended period of time, dosage should be stopped gradually and only under the care and advice of a physician.
