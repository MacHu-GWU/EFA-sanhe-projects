  • vomiting

  • unusual dreams
  • daytime drowsiness
  • dizziness
  • lightheadedness
  • heartburn
  • unpleasant taste
  • dry mouth
  • decreased sexual desire
  • painful menstrual periods
  • breast enlargement in males

Warnings & Recalls for Lunesta

  • Lunesta has been labelled a federally controlled substance due to its increased risk for abuse and dependence.
  • Those with a history of depression, mental illness, lung disease, liver disease, and drug or alcohol abuse should consult a healthcare physician before taking this medication to avoid any serious negative side effects.
  • Elderly patients should be monitored carefully, as they have a higher sensitivity to side effects cause by Lunesta.
  • Lunesta should only be taken when absolutely needed during pregnancy, as taking this medication close to the due date can cause unwanted side effects. Those who are breastfeeding should consult with their doctor before continuing the use of Lunesta, as it is unknown whether this medication can be passed into the breast milk.
  • While taking Lunesta, some patients have gotten out of bed without being fully awake, and committed acts that they were unaware they were doing, and don't remember the next morning. The risk of these acts is increased when Lunesta is combined with alcohol or other medications. If you experience any of these symptoms, immediately consult your doctor.
