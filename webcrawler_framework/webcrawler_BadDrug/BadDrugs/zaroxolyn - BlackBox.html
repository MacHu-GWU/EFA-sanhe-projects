Zaroxolyn Side Effects

Serious Side Effects of Zaroxolyn

  • Get medical attention immediately if you experience a fever accompanied by a sore throat, unexplained bruising or bleeding, severe, peeling rashes, or have a hard time breathing or swallowing.
  • Dry mouth, excessive thirst, vomiting, and nausea may signs of a serious reaction. Contact your doctor if you experience these side effects.
  • If you feel excessive weakness, drowsiness, restlessness, or light-headedness, stop taking Zaroxolyn and call your doctor.
  • Talk to a medical professional if you experience numbness, an abnormal heartbeat, or pain in the chest.

Common Side effects of Zaroxolyn

  • Taking Zaroxolyn may cause drowsiness. Do not do anything that requires your complete attention (including driving) until you know how this medication will effect you. Alcohol can intensify this drowsiness.
  • Avoid exposure to sunlight while taking Zaroxolyn. It may make you more sensitive to the sun.

Warnings & Recalls for Zaroxolyn

Zaroxolyn Warnings

  • Let your doctor know if you have a history of gout, diabetes, or any type of liver, thyroid, or kidney disease.
  • Other formulations for metolazone are not substitutable for each other.
  • If you have a difficult time urinating or acute liver disease, you should not take Zaroxolyn.
  • Avoid dehydration or overheating while taking Zaroxolyn and drink an appropriate amount of liquid.
  • Do not take Zaroxolyn if you are breastfeeding. It may pass into the infant. However, there is not evidence that Zaroxolyn is harmful to unborn children.
  • Avoid medications that can also make you light-headed. They can intensify the side effects of Zaroxolyn.

Zaroxolyn Interactions

  • Lithium medications, digoxin, methenamines, steroid medications, diabetic medicines, blood thinners, blood pressure medicine, salicylates, and NSAIDs may affect how Zaroxolyn works in the body. Let your doctor know if you are taking any of these types of drugs.

Zaroxolyn Treatment and Use

Zaroxolyn | Metolazone is a diuretic, or water pill, that is used to control high blood pressure and treat water retention caused by various ailments. It may also be used as a treatment for diabetes insipidus and a preventative treatment for kidney stones for patients with high calcium levels. It is also sometimes used to decrease certain symptoms like trouble breathing.

How does Zaroxolyn work?

Zaroxolyn encourages your kidneys to get rid of excess water and sodium and increases your body’s urine production. It inhibits the liver’s ability to reabsorb electrolytes, so sodium is discharges in a more normalized manner. This also prevents edema, or fluid retention in patients with congestive heart failure, or kidney disorders.

Zaroxolyn Dosage Information

This tablet medication is typically taken once daily in the morning. Food does not affect Zaroxolyn, so it may be taken with or without a meal. Taking this medication later in the day may disrupt your sleeping patterns by causing you to wake up to urinate.

To achieve the best results, Zaroxolyn should be taken regularly, even if you you feel fine. Blood pressure improvement may take up to six weeks.

When taking Zaroxolyn, your doctor may ask you to exercise and take part in a diet low in sodium but rich in potassium.
