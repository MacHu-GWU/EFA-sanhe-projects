Imitrex Side Effects

Imitrex | Sumatriptan has been shown to cause side effects such as
  • mild headache
  • feelings of pressure or heaviness in any part of the body
  • hot or cold feeling
  • dizziness
  • spinning sensation
  • drowsiness
  • nausea
  • vomiting
  • drooling
  • warm, redness, or tingling under the skin
  • unusual taste in mouth (nasal spray)
  • burning, numbness, pain, or irritation in the nose of throat (nasal spray)

Warnings & Recalls for Imitrex

Imitrex should not be used by patients with a history of any of the following conditions:
  • heart disease
  • coronary heart disease
  • angina
  • blood circulation issues
  • lack of blood supply to the heart
  • uncontrolled high blood pressure
  • severe liver disease
  • ischemic bowel disease
  • history of heart attack or stroke
Consult your doctor before taking Imitrex if you suffer from any of the following diseases:
  • liver disease
  • kidney disease
  • epilepsy or seizure disorder
  • high blood pressure
  • heart rhythm disorder
  • coronary heart disease
Patients who are pregnant or breastfeeding should consult a doctor before taking this medication, as it is unknown whether this medication can cause harm to an unborn child.

Imitrex Treatment and Use

Imitrex | Sumatriptan is a selective serotonin receptor agonist manufactured by GlaxoSmithKline as an oral tablet and intranasal solution. Imitrex not only blocks pain signals from being interpreted by the brain, but also constricts blood vessels in the head to relieve the symptoms of migraines.

Imitrex | Sumatriptan has been approved for the treatment of migraines and common tension headaches, and is used to treat such symptoms as:

  • sensitivity to light or sound
  • nausea
  • vomiting

Imitrex should not be used for the prevention of future migraines, or to decrease the frequency of headaches a patient experiences.
