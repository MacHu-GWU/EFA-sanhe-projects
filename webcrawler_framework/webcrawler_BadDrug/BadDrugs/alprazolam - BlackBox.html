Alprazolam Side Effects

Alprazolam has been shown to cause common side effects such as:
  • drowsiness
  • dizziness
  • fatigue
  • irritability
  • blurred vision
  • headache
  • memory problems
  • difficulty concentrating
  • insomnia
  • swelling in the hands or feet
  • muscle weakness
  • lack of coordination
  • slurred speed
  • nausea
  • vomiting
  • constipation
  • diarrhea
  • increased sweating
  • dry mouth
  • stuffy noise
  • appetite or weight change
Alprazolam may also cause more serious side effects, such as:
  • depressed mood
  • suicidal thoughts
  • confusion
  • hyperactivity
  • agitation
  • hostility
  • hallucinations
  • felling like you might pass out
  • chest pain
  • decreased frequency of urination
  • chest pain
  • pounding heartbeat
  • uncontrolled muscle movements or tremor
  • seizures
  • jaundice
If you experience any of these serious side effects, or if symptoms persist, stop taking Alprazolam and consult your doctor immediately.

Warnings & Recalls for Alprazolam

Alprazolam should not be taken by patients who:
  • suffer from narrow-angle glaucoma
  • are taking itraconazole or ketoconazole
  • are allergic to alprazolam or other benzodiazepines

Alprazolam Treatment and Use

Alprazolam is a generic benzodiazepine medication which is manufactured as a tablet, extended-release tablet, orally disintegrating tablet, and a concentrated solution. Alprazolam works to increase the effects of neurotransmitters that slow the irregular movement of chemicals in the brain that can cause feelings of fear and worry.

Alprazolam has been approved for the treatment of:

  • Anxiety disorders
  • Panic disorders
  • Anxiety resulting from depression

The dosage you are prescribed is based on your medical history and the level of effectiveness that you experience after taking this drug.
