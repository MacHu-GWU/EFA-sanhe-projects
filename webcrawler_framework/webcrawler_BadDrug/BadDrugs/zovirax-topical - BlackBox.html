Zovirax Topical Side Effects

Serious Side Effects of Zovirax Topical

  • Symptoms of an allergic reaction to Zovirax Topical include swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat, difficulty breating, and hives.
  • If any of the common side effects become severe, contact your doctor.

Common Side Effects of Zovirax Topical

  • When applied to the outer mouth, you may experience dryness, cracking or peeling of the lips.
  • Treated areas of the skin  may become dry or begin to flake.
  • After application you may feel burning, stinging, or itching sensations.

Warnings & Recalls for Zovirax Topical

General Zovirax Topical Warnings

  • Do not get Zovirax Topical in your eyes, mouth, or nose.
  • Zovirax Topical will not stop the spread of genital herpes. You can still infect other people while using this medication. Avoid sexual intercourse or use protection to prevent spreading the infection.
  • Let your doctor know if your immune system has been weakened

Zovirax Topical Treatment and Use

Zovirax Topical | acyclovir topical is used to treat cold sores and genital herpes lesions, and slows the spread of the herpes virus to help the infection heal. It is only used to treat the symptoms of  herpes and will not cure the infection or prevent it in the future.

How does Zovirax Topical work?

Zovirax Topical is an antiviral medication that actively works against the herpes simplex virus (HSV) by interfering with how the virus replicates.

Zovirax Topical Dosage Information

Even if your symptoms improve before your prescribed dosage is over, you should continue to use Zovirax Topical for the full dosage length. The treatment should begin as soon as you see symptoms of herpes.

Zovirax Topical can be applied up to six times a day for seven days.

Wash your hands before and after using Zovirax Topical. To prevent spreading the virus, wear a glove or use a cotton swab to apply the cream. Use only enough to cover the affected area and rub gently until you can no longer see the ointment.
