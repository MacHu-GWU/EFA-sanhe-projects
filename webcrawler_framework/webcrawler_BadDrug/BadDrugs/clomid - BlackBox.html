Clomid Side Effects

Serious Adverse Side Effects of Clomid

There are serious adverse side effects associated with taking this drug:
  • blurred vision
  • visual spots or flashes
  • double vision
  • stomach or lower stomach pain
  • stomach swelling
  • weight gain
  • shortness of breath
Long-term use of Clomid | Clomiphene may increase the risk of ovarian cancer. This drug should not be used for more than about six cycles.

Common Adverse Side Effects of Clomid

Lesser and more common adverse side effects of this drug are:
  • enlargement of the ovaries
  • flushing (feeling of warmth)
  • upset stomach
  • vomiting
  • breast discomfort
  • headache
  • abnormal vaginal bleeding
If you or a loved one has experienced serious adverse side effects as a result of taking this drug, please do not hesitate to contact someone who can help you.

Warnings & Recalls for Clomid

Warnings and precautions before using Clomid | Clomiphene are:
  • Do not use Clomid while driving or operating machinery, as Clomid may impair vision
  • That multiple pregnancies can occur, which can result in more than one fetus
  • Ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome may occur
  • Abnormal ovarian enlargement may occur
  • Increased risk for ovarian cancer
There are associated contraindications before taking Clomid | Clomiphene:
  • Do not take while pregnant
  • Uncontrolled thyroid or adrenal dysfunction
  • Liver disease
  • Abnormal uterine bleeding
  • Endometrial carcinoma
  • Ovarian cysts
  • Organic intracranial lesions
  • Hypersensitivity to Clomiphene, which is the active ingredient in this drug

Clomid Treatment and Use

Clomid | Clomiphene is a selective estrogen receptor modulator (SERM) medication that is used to induce ovulation in women who do not normally produce eggs, or ova, but wish to become pregnant. It works very similarly to estrogen, which is a female hormone that causes eggs to develop in the ovaries and be released.

The official FDA indications for Clomid | Clomiphene are for:

  • Female infertility due to ovulatory disorder
  • Infertility due to polycystic ovary syndrome

Non-FDA labeled indications for this drug are for:

  • Clomiphene test
  • Endocrine female infertility from hyperandrogenemia
  • Female infertility due to a luteal phase defect
  • Idiopathic infertility
  • In vitro fertilization
  • Male hypogonadism

Clomid | Clomiphene comes as a pill to take orally during a cycle of successive days.

How Does Clomid Work?

Clomid is an ovulatory stimulant that blocks estrogen by attaching to estrogen-receptors in the brain. This causes the body to produce more estrogen in order to compensate for low estrogen levels, and, ideally, induces ovulation.

Adverse Side Effects of Clomid

There are serious adverse side effects that result from taking Clomid | Clomiphene, which can range from reproductive side effects to gastrointestinal; in some extreme cases Clomid may cause breast cancer.
