Trazodone Side Effects

Serious Side Effects of Trazodone

  • increased depression
  • increased thoughts of suicide
  • drastic changes in mood
  • trouble sleeping
  • severe dizziness or passing out
  • increase in heart rate or difficulty breathing
  • hallucinating
  • feeling jittery, irritated or angry, tense muscles, rapid heartbeats and faintness
  • nauseousness or throwing up
  • diarrhea
  • seizure
  • bruising or bleeding easily
  • pain spreading from the chest, arm and shoulder
  • allergic reaction: redness or itchiness of the skin, swelling of the throat, mouth, tongue or face, and trouble breathing

Common Side Effects of Trazodone

  • tiredness
  • minor headache
  • constipation
  • changes in vision

Warnings & Recalls for Trazodone

Trazodone may be addictive if used longer than recommended or at higher doses. Talk to your doctor if you feel that Trazodone is not working as effectively as before and you feel the need to take more. Do not use more Trazodone than prescribed. Taking more than instructed could result in an overdose or even death.Patients under the age of 24 who use Trazodone may be at risk of increased depression or suicide. If you are taking Trazodone and have increased thoughts of suicide, contact your doctor immediately. Patients may be asked to regularly visit their doctor.To ensure your safety while using Trazodone, let your doctor know if you have:
  • liver disease
  • kidney disease
  • or have ever had Long QT syndrom (LQTS)
  • had a heart attack
  • heart disease or disorder
  • any other mental illnesses
  • diabetes
Trazodone is an FDA pregnancy Category C medication. It is not known if taking Trazodone while pregnant will harm the fetus. When tested on animals, Trazodone caused an increase in miscarriages and birth defects. Trazodone can pass through breast-milk to a nursing baby. If you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant or are breastfeeding, talk to your doctor before taking Trazodone.In 2009, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration rejected Trazodone once-daily pills due to deficiencies in the factory where the medication was being produced. Manufactureres plan to release a similar pill in the future.

Trazodone Drug Interactions

Do not take narcotics, sedatives or any other medicines that may increase drowsiness. The combination of these drugs with Trazodone may be dangerous.Talk to your doctor about safely using pain relievers with Trazodone. Together these drugs can cause you to bruise or bleed easier than normal.Avoid drinking alcohol while taking  Trazodone. This may cause severe dizziness or fatigue. Do not drive or operate machinery if you are under the influence of alcohol.Blood thinners, antidepressants, antibiotics, and anti-fungal, blood pressure or heart, HIV/AIDS, anti-nausea, migraine and seizure medications can all interact with Trazodone. Before taking Trazodone, tell your doctor about any medications you are taking. 

Trazodone Treatment and Use

Trazodone | Desyrel, Oleptro is a generic serotonin modulator medication used for treating symptoms of depression. Trazadone can be used to treat the following:

  • inability to sleep
  • schizophrenia
  • uncontrollable movements
  • nervousness or anxiety

Trazodone is not a cure for depression. Trazodone should not be used as an “everyday” anxiety reliever.

How Does Trazodone Work?

Trazodone is thought to stabilize activity of serotonin in the brain that may have become unbalanced during depression. Increased levels of serotonin may lead to improving overall mood or confidence.

Trazodone Dosage Information

Trazodone is given in tablet form to be taken orally with a full glass of water. Trazodone should be taken with food  two or more times each day as recommended by your doctor. Read and follow all instructions carefully. To allow your body and hormones to adjust to Trazodone, your doctor may gradually increase or decrease doses when starting or ending use of this medicine.

It may take 14 days or more to see results from Trazodone.

Do not end your use of Trazodone unless instructed to do so by your doctor. Even if you feel healthy, you may still need to use Trazodone.
