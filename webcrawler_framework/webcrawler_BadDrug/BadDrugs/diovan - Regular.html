Diovan Side Effects

Diovan | valsartan side effects and adverse reactions include:
  • Cough
  • Fatigue
  • Dizziness
  • Low blood pressure (especially if you take water pills)
  • Increase in potassium
  • Joint pain
  • Back pain
  • Stomach problems
  • Vertigo
  • Severe allergic reaction
  • Anxiety
  • Rhabdomyolysis
  • Hepatitis
  • Increased urination (Diovan HCT)

Warnings & Recalls for Diovan

Tell your doctor if you have/are:
  • Gallstones
  • Any food or medication allergies
  • Kidney disease
  • Pregnant or nursing
  • On kidney dialysis

Diovan Treatment and Use

Diovan | valsartan is an angiotensin II receptor antagonist made by Novartis that is indicated for the treatment of hypertension as well as heart failure in patients whose bodies cannot tolerate drugs called angiotensin converting enzyme inhibitors. This medications are either used alone or in combination with other drugs to lower blood pressure in patients who take Diovan

How does Diovan work?

Diovan is an angiotensin II inhibitor. Angiotensin II causes narrowing of the blood vessels. By inhibiting this chemical from being produced, Diovan allows the blood vessels to be wider and more relaxed, thus allowing your blood to flow more easily and your blood pressure to decrease.

Diovan dosage

  • Diovan comes in tablets of 80 mg, 160 mg, and 320 mg. It can be taken with or without food and once daily.
  • The recommended starting dose for Diovan is 80 mg-160 mg. This can be increased to 320 mg if directed by a doctor.
  • Care should be taken when prescribing this drug to persons with severe renal impairments.
