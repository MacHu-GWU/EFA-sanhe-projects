Zyvox Side Effects

Zyvox | linezolid has been known to cause side effects such as
  • nausea
  • vomiting
  • headache
  • dizziness
  • diarrhea
  • stomach pain
  • difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep
  • white patches in the mouth
  • discoloration of the tongue

Warnings & Recalls for Zyvox

  • Zyvox should not be used if you have previously used or are currently using any MAO inhibitor medications.
  • You should not start using any new medications while taking Zyvox, nor should you discontinue the use of any medications unless specified by your doctor.
  • Avoid eating foods with high levels of tyramine, as these can dangerously raise your blood pressure.
  • Zyvox has a received a pregnancy Category C, and it should not be taken while pregnant or breastfeeding without consulting a doctor. The effects of Zyvox on a newborn or nursing baby are unknown.

Zyvox Treatment and Use

Zyvox | linezolid is an oxazolidinones antibacterial medication manufactured by Pfizer as a tablet and oral suspension liquid. By halting the growth of antibiotic-resistant strains of bacteria, Zyvox is able to treat infections caused by Gram-positive bacteria.

Zyvox has been approved for the treatment of bacteria such as

  • Streptococcus pneumoniae
  • Staphylococcus aureus
  • Skin and soft tissue infections, such as those caused by S. pyogenes or S. aureus
  • Pneumonia
  • Vancomycin-resistant Enterococcus infection
