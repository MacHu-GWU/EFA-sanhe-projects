Nifedical Side Effects

Serious Adverse Effects of Nifedical

Serious adverse side effects of Nifedical | Nifedipine include:
  • Cardiovascular side effects such as heart attacks and ventricular arrhythmia
  • Gastrointestinal side effects such as gastrointestinal obstruction and ulcers
  • Hematological side effects such as aplastic anemia

Moderate Adverse Side Effects of Nifedical

Less serious and more common side effects of Nifedical | Nifedipine include:
  • Cardiovascular side effects such as hypotension, palpitations, and peripheral edema
  • Dermatological side effects such as flushing
  • Gastrointestinal side effects such as nausea
  • Neurological side effects such as dizziness and headache
  • Psychiatric side effects such as anxiety
  • Respiratory side effects such as cough and dyspnea

Warnings & Recalls for Nifedical

Contraindications of Nifedical

Contraindications of Nifedical | Nifedipine include cardiogenic shock, concomitant use with strong P450 inducers, or a hypersensitivity to the ingredients of Nifedical | Nifedipine.

Warnings and Precautions of Nifedical

Some warnings and precautions before taking Nifedical | Nifedipine are:
  • Narrowing of the aortic valve, which can increase risk of heart failure
  • Disruption in the flow of bile to the liver, with or without jaundice
  • Liver scarring or inhibited liver function
  • Concomitant use with CYP3A inducers should not be used with Nifedical | Nifedipine
  • Concomitant use with flecainide
  • Concomitant use with beta-blockers, which can induce aortic stenosis
  • Galactose intolerance, Lapp-lactase deficiency, or glucose-galactose malabsorption
  • Gastrointestinal mass or obstruction formation, which can lead to serious surgery, and gastrointestinal ulceration
  • Grapefruit juice may interfere with the effectiveness of this medication
  • Liver impairment or hepatitis
  • Obstructive coronary artery disease, which leads to an increased risk of chest pain exacerbation or heart attack when it comes to increasing the dose
  • Kidney insuffiency
  • Surgery with high-dose fentanyl anesthesia

Nifedical Treatment and Use

Nifedical | Nifedipine is a drug that is prescribed to treat high blood pressure and is also used to control angina, or chest pain.  Nifedical is classified as a calcium-channel blocker and works by relaxing blood vessels so that the heart of a patient does not have to pump as hard.  It also incrementally increases the supply of oxygen and blood to the heart of the patient.  Nifedical | Nifedipine is also used to treat preterm labor, as well as Raynaud’s syndrome.

Off-Label Uses of Nifedical

Nifedical is commonly prescribed off-label to treat the following conditions:

  • Autonomic dysreflexia
  • Addressing cardiovascular event risks during surgical procedures of the heart
  • Chilblains, or the inflammation of blood vessels beneath the skin
  • Diffuse spasm of the esophagus
  • Disorders of the anus
  • External cephalic version with tocolysis
  • High altitude pulmonary edema
  • Hypertension during pregnancy
  • Hypertension during transplantation
  • Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy
  • Pulmonary hypertension
  • Renovascular hypertension
  • Kidney stones

Teva Pharmaceuticals is the drug company that makes and markets Nifedical | Nifedipine.
