Opana ER Side Effects

Opana ER side effects and adverse reactions include the following:
  • constipation
  • loss of appetite
  • diarrhea
  • dizziness
  • fatigue
  • dry mouth
  • nausea and vomiting
  • insomnia
  • allergic reaction
  • breathing difficulties | respiratory depression
  • hallucinations
  • chest pain
  • confusion
  • fainting
  • irregular heartbeat
  • fever
This is not a complete list of Opana ER side effects. You may experience none or multiple adverse reactions from taking this drug. Warnings & Recalls for Opana ER

  • Tell your doctor about the following conditions:
    • diseases of the pancreas or gallbladder
    • gastrointestinal disorders
    • a history of addiction. Opana ER is a controlled substance and carries with it the risk of dependency.
    • alcohol use or abuse
    • liver disease
    • respiratory problems (lung disease)
    • enlarged prostate
    • if you are pregnant, nursing, or thinking of becoming pregnant
  • Be sure to also tell your doctor about any food or medicine allergies you have as well as any previous or current medical conditions.
  • Do not ingest alcohol while on this medication.
  • This medication may cause severe respiratory depression. It is contraindicated in patients with lung disease or current respiratory depressive symptoms.

Opana ER Treatment and Use

Opana ER | oxymorphone is a partially synthetic opioid treatment that is used to relieve the symptoms of chronic severe pain and extreme pain after surgery. Due partly to the risk of Opana abuse as well as other Opana ER side effects, this drug is not to be used to treat pain on an “as needed” basis. Nor is Opana ER to be used to treat mild pain that may occur shortly after a surgery.

How does Opana ER work?

Opana ER works as other opioid drugs do. It changes the way the body perceives pain by attaching to certain receptors and blocking the pain sensation from reaching the brain. This action is coupled with a feeling of euphoria that is similar to other opioid drugs like morphine.

Opana ER side effects

Opana ER carries with it the risk of addiction. Opana ER’s active ingredient is the opioid oxymorphone. The risk of Opana ER dependency and addiction should be taken into consideration by both the doctor prescribing the medication as well as the patient being treated with it.

If you begin to feel a dependence on Opana ER or any other substance, seek professional help and utilize addiction resources like the National Addiction Hotline at 1(866)-301-0367.

Opana ER dosage

  • Opana ER comes as a tablet and should be swallowed whole on an empty stomach at least one to two hours before eating.
  • The use of alcohol is contraindicated in patients receiving treatment with Opana ER.
  • Opana ER dosage is to be individualized for each patient and is dependent on the patient’s condition, medical history, as well as other factors a doctor will take into consideration when writing a prescription for Opana ER.
  • If you have any questions about Opana ER dosage, or about Opana ER dependency and side effects, be sure to speak to your doctor to receive clarification.
