Pulmozyme Side Effects

There can be a variety of adverse side effects as a result of taking Pulmozyme | Dornase Alfa.  Adverse effects such as:
  • Cardiovascular: Chest pain
  • Dermatalogic: Rashes on the skin
  • Ophthalmic: Conjunctivitis (Pink eye)
  • Respiratory: Laryngitis, pharyngitis

Pulmozyme Treatment and Use

Pulmozyme | Dornase Alfa is a drug that’s been developed by the drug company Genentech.  It’s a highly purified solution of recombinant human deoxyribonuclease I (rhDNase), which is an enzyme that selectively latches onto DNA.  This drug is used to treat cystic fibrosis in patients, but doesn’t cure it.  Pulmozyme | Dornase Alfa hydrolyzes DNA present in the mucus of cystic fibrosis patients and thereby reduces the viscosity of the lungs which in turn promotes improved clearance of secretions.  This highly purified solution is derived from Chinese hamster ovary cells.

Pulmozyme | Dornase Alfa comes as a solution to be inhaled through the mouth and taken about once or twice a day.
