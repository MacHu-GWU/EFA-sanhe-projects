Warnings & Recalls for Antivert

Tell your doctor about the following before you begin treatment with Antivert:
  • asthma
  • glaucoma
  • allergies to food or medication
  • enlarged prostate
  • urinary problems
  • if you are pregnant, nursing, think you may be pregnant, or are considering becoming pregnant

Antivert Treatment and Use

Antivert | meclizine is an antiemetic antihistamine medication that is used to treat the nausea and vomiting associated with motion sickness as well as treatment for Meniere’s disease, an inner ear disorder. This medication may also be given to decrease lightheadedness, vertigo, and dizziness, as well as to treat symptoms of conditions not indicated by the FDA.

How does Antivert work?

Though Antivert | meclizine is an antihistamine, it is usually not used for these properties. Instead Antivert is useful for its anticholinergic activity that counters the symptoms of nausea and vomiting that come with motion sickness.

Antivert Dosage

Dosage of Antivert depends on the conditions (vertigo, Meniere’s disease, motion sickness) treated, as well as your personal medical history. Your doctor will take these into account when directing you to be treated by Antivert | meclizine.

  • Use this medication exactly as directed by your physician for the condition that is to be treated.
  • Antivert comes as a coated tablet, a chewable tablet, and a capsule.
  • For motion sickness, this drug should be taken one hour before you embark on an activity that will give you motion sickness.
  • Treatment of vertigo with Antivert may require several daily doses of the medication.
  • Ask your doctor any questions you may have about being treated with Antivert.
