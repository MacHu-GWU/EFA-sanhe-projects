Proventil HFA Side Effects

Serious Side Effects of Proventil HFA

  • pain or tension in the chest
  • rapid or abnormal heart rate
  • trouble breathing which could indicate a paradoxical bronchospasm
  • sores in the mouth
  • worsened side effects of a breathing disorder
  • strong headache
  • feeling extremely dizzy
  • hoarseness or wheezing
  • severe allergic reaction: rash, hives, swelling of the tongue, mouth, throat, face, and trouble breathing

Common Side Effects Caused By Proventil HFA

  • coughing, soreness of the throat, and sinus irritation
  • mild headache or feeling anxious
  • dryness of the throat or mouth
  • strange or unpleasant taste in mouth
  • upset stomach, nausea, or throwing up
  • feeling unsteady or shaky
  • difficulty sleeping

Warnings & Recalls for Proventil HFA

Proventil HFA and Pregnancy

Proventil HFA has been assigned by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration as a pregnancy Category C drug. It is unknown whether Proventil HFA may cause harm to the fetus if taken by a pregnant woman; however, when tested in pregnant animals, there was an increased risk of birth defects including cleft palate, and problems with the brain and skull formation. It is not known if Proventil HFA could pass through breast-milk and cause harm to a nursing baby.If you are pregnant, may become pregnant or are breastfeeding, discuss the risks and benefits of using Proventil HFA to determine if this medicine is safe to use.

General Warnings

If your condition continues or symptoms become worse, be sure to tell your doctor. Your asthma may be worsening. Call your doctor immediately, especially if side effects increase directly after using Proventil HFA.Do not use more than the dose prescribed by your doctor. Taking more Proventil HFA than recommended can lead to serious heart issues and even death.Elderly patients may be more prone to the side effects caused by Proventil HFA.Do not take Proventil HFA if you:
  • are allergic to Proventil HFA or any ingredient used in this medication
  • use a different immediate-relief sympathomimetic bronchodulator
  • are under the age of 4 unless prescribed by your doctor
Let your healthcare provider know about any of the following:
  • any allergies you may have
  • medicines, vitamins, supplements or herbal products you use
  • if you have ever had heart issues, blood vessel irregularities, high blood pressure (hypertension) or low potassium levels in the blood
  • if you have a history of any condition causing seizures
  • diabetes
  • overactive thyroid
  • kidney disease or failure
  • tumor of the adrenal gland

Proventil Interactions

Avoid drinking alcohol while using Proventil HFA. When combined these drugs may cause severe dizziness and could be unsafe during daily routines.Proventil HFA can alter blood sugar levels. If you have diabetes, ask your doctor how to safely use Proventil HFA.Be sure to tell all healthcare providers that you are taking Proventil HFA. Also tell them about any other medicines you are using. The following may interact with Proventil HFA:
  • diuretics
  • monoamine oxidase (MAO) inhibitors
  • tricyclic antidepressants
  • diuretics
  • catechol-omethyltransferase (COMT) inhibitors for Parkinson disease
  • quick-relief bronchodilators or sympathomimetics
  • stimulants
  • beta-blockers
  • digoxin
 

Proventil HFA Treatment and Use

Proventil HFA | albuterol sulfate is a beta-adrenergic receptor agonist (beta agonist) bronchodilator marketed by Schering-Plough for preventing and reducing breathing issues related to respiratory diseases and conditions such as asthma. Proventil HFA may be prescribed for:

  • preventing wheezing, shortness of breath, or coughing
  • treating symptoms of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
  • preventing asthma attacks caused by exercise

How Does Proventil HFA Work?

During an asthma attack, the airways are constricted by muscles lining the passage. Proventil HFA works by interacting with beta receptors in the body and signaling the relaxation and widening of the muscles. This allows for a greater amount of air to enter the lungs when breathing.

Proventil HFA Dosage Information

Proventil HFA comes in the form of a metered-dose inhaler (MDI) and is approved for use in patients over the age of 4 years old. Dosage depends on the condition being treated or prevented. Ask your doctor which dose amount is right for you. Follow all instructions given to you by your doctor.

For preventing an asthma attack patients are commonly prescribed to take Proventil HFA in two sprays per dose on an as needed basis. No more than one Proventil HFA dose should be taken in 4 hours. Ask your doctor if you feel the need to use more Proventil HFA than previously prescribed.

Proventil HFA treatment to prevent an asthma attack brought on by exercise is as a two spray dose to be taken between 15 minutes and 30 minutes ahead of scheduled exercise.

To take Proventil HFA:

  • spray the inhaler into the air twice to prime the pump if you have not used the inhaler within the last two weeks
  • shake the canister before using
  • exhale completely then hold the inhaler up to your mouth and firmly press down on the canister as you breathe in steadily. Hold your breath for several seconds before exhaling.
  • rinse your mouth out with water
  • wait a minimum of 60 seconds before inhaling more Proventil HFA
  • clean the mouthpiece of the inhaler once every week
  • Proventil HFA should be thrown away after 20o sprays

Always have Proventil HFA ready to use at the onset of an asthma attack.

Do not stop using Proventil HFA unless your doctor recommends that you end your treatment.
