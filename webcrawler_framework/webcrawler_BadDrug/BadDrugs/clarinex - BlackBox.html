Clarinex Side Effects

Serious Side Effects of Clarinex

  • yellowing of the skin or the eyes (jaundice)
  • seizures
  • flu-like symptoms such as a sore throat, fever, and chills
  • changes in heart rate including irregular beats or a rapid heart rate
  • swelling of the mouth, lips, tongue, or face, trouble breathing, hoarseness, rash or hives which indicate a serious allergic reaction

Common Side Effects of Clarinex

  • dryness of the mouth
  • coughing or soreness of the throat
  • aching or pain in the muscles
  • changes in bowels such as diarrhea
  • stomach ache and nauseousness
  • headache
  • fatigue or tiredness

Warnings & Recalls for Clarinex

Pregnancy and Clarinex

Clarinex has been assigned as an FDA pregnancy Category C medicine. Research does not confirm if Clarinex may harm the fetus or not if taken during pregnancy. Clarinex can be passed through breast-milk and could harm a baby that is nursing. Patients who are breastfeeding should not take Clarinex without consulting their doctor. If you are pregnant or may become pregnant talk to your doctor before using Clarinex.

General Warnings

Be sure to tell your doctor about any medications you are using or any medical conditions you may have, especially kidney or liver disease, or phenylketonuria (PKU syndrome, a metabolic genetic disorder).Patients should know that Clarinex RediTabs could contain phenylalanine. If you are allergic to any ingredient of Clarinex, do not use it as a treatment for your allergies.

Clarinex Treatment and Use

Clarinex | Desloratadine belongs to a group of medicines called antihistamines. Clarinex was approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration in February of 2002 and is marketed by Value Pharmaceuticals for reducing allergy symptoms such as:

  • runny nose or sneezing
  • itchy nose or eyes
  • watering of the eyes

Clarinex may also be prescribed for treating skin problems such as hives or rashes in patients with chronic skin allergic reactions.

How Does Clarinex Work?

Histamines in the body are responsible for binding to receptors and causing inflammation and other symptoms during an allergy. Clarinex works by keeping histamines from attaching to receptors, causing a reduction in swelling and other side effects of allergies.

Clarinex is not for patients under two years old unless otherwise prescribed by a doctor.

How to Use Clarinex

Clarinex is available in tablet, liquid (oral suspension), and disintegrating tablet (RediTabs) form. Clarinex should be taken by mouth typically once each day with or without food. Clarinex tablets come in 5mg doses and RediTabs come in 2.5 mg and 5mg doses. Use only the amount prescribed to you.

If you are using the oral suspension be sure to pour the liquid into a proper dose-measuring device.

Swallow tablets whole without crushing or breaking the medicine.

To use Clarinex RediTabs, peel back the foil of the blister-pack with dry hands and remove the tablet. Place the tablet on your tongue and allow Clarinex to dissolve, then swallow the disintegrated tablet.
