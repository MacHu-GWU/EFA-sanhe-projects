Taclonex Side Effects

Serious Side Effects of Taclonex

  • If your skin becomes severely irritated or your psoriasis symptoms get worse stop taking Taclonex and talk to your doctor as soon as possible.
  • Symptoms of infection include pus, swelling, and persistent itchiness. If you suspect that your skin has become infected, contact your doctor immediately.
  • Get medical attention if you suddenly become confused, extremely tired, or lose your appetite.
  • Symptoms of Cushing Syndrome are unexplained weight gain, brusing easily, thinning of the skin, an increased amount of facial hair, or darkening of the skin.
  • Taclonex may cause high blood sugar. Signs of high blood sugar include an increased need to urinate, excessive thirst, nausea, and vomiting.
  • When too much of the corticosteroid reaches your bloodstream you may become unusually tired, rapidly lose weight, develop swelling of the feet or ankles, and have trouble with your vision. Stop tking Taclonex and get medical attention if you experience any of these symptoms.
  • Taclonex may cause your calcium levels to rise too much. If become constipated or notice an unexplained change in your mood, tell your doctor as soon as possible.

Common Side Effects of Taclonex

  • Taclonex may make you more sensitive to sunlight after application. Avoid being in the sun for extended periods of time to avoid getting a sunburn.
  • You may experience mild burning or itching after applying Taclonex. You may also develop a scaly rash in the area of application.
  • Taclonex may cause your skin to change color or your hair follicles to swell after use.

Warnings & Recalls for Taclonex

Taclonex Warnings

  • If you have abnormal levels of calcium, a history of liver or kidney disorders, a skin infection, or a very severe type of psoriasis your doctor may not recommend Taclonex for you.
  • Talk to your doctor about any UV light treatment you may be receiving to treat your psoriasis.
  • Taclonex should not be used to treat children.
  • Tell your doctor if you are taking steroid medications or any other psoriasis medications.
  • If you are pregnant or plan on becoming pregnant, have a discussion with your doctor about whether or not you should take Taclonex.

Taclonex Treatment and Use

Taclonex | Betamethasone and Calcipotriene is a medication used to treat symptoms of psoriasis vulgaris or plaque psoriasis in adults. It combines two common psoriasis treatments into one medication. Taclonex helps reduce itching, inflamation, and redness of the skin affected by psoriasis. This helps the skin become more healthy and decreases the risk of infection. Some patients see improvement within the first week of treatment. It has not been adequately tested in patients under 18 years old.

How does Taclonex Work?

The Betamethasone in Taclonex is a corticosteroid. It helps decrease the itching and swelling symptoms of psoriasis. Calcipoterine is a type of vitamin D that inhibits the growth of skin cells, preventing thickening of the skin.

Taclonex Dosage Information

Do not exceed the dosage or length of Taclonex treatment that your doctor prescribes for you. Apply Taclonex to the area that needs treatment once daily for up to four weeks. Your doctor may require blood or urine tests to monitor your condition while you use Taclonex.

Make sure your hands are clean before you use Taclonex. Shake liquid Taclonex before application and apply a thin layer to the affected area. Do not apply Taclonex on skin that is infected, wounded, broken, dry, or thin. Taclonex should not be used on your face, underarms, or groin. Do not let the product come in contact with your eyes or mouth.
