Common Side Effects of Dymista

  • Dymista, like most allergy medications, has been known to cause drowsiness and headache.
  • This medication may irritate the nasal cavity and cause minor nosebleeds, and may drip down into the mouth and alter your sense of taste.
  • Sinus pain, cough, and sore throat may also occur while using Dymista.

Warnings & Recalls for Dymista

General Dymista Warnings

  • If you are allergic to Azelastine or Fluticasone, or are taking Ritonavir, you should not use this medication.
  • Let your doctor know if you have glaucoma, cararacts, herpes of the eye, tuberculosis, or any other infections, sores, or ulsers in your nose.
  • Steroids may increase the glucose in your body so if you have diabetes you may have to adjust the dose of your medications.
  • Fluticasone can decrease the number of blood cells that help fight infections. Avoid being around people who are sick. If you are exposed to chicken pox or measles call your doctor for treatment.

Dymista Interactions

Dymista should not be used with alcohol or other central neverous system depressants to prevent impairment of central nervous system performance. Other medications that cause drowsiness include:
  • antipsychotic, anxiety, and depression medications
  • Muscle relaxants
  • Narcotic pain relievers
  • Sleeping medications
  • and some antihistamines.
  • This medication may interact adversely to CYP 3A4 inhibitors.
  • When used with Ritonavir gushing syndrome and adrenal suppression may occur.

How does Dymista Work?

Dymista is a combination of azelastine and fluticasone. Azelastine is an antihistamine that reduces the effects of a naturally occurring compound known as histamine, which is produced during allergic reactions and causes increased mucus production. Fluticasone’s exact mechanism of action is unknown; it is a steroid medication that is thought to prevent inflammation by inhibiting the release of chemicals which cause the inflammatory response.

Dymista Dosage Information

It may take a few days before the body reacts to the medication and symptoms improve.

To ensure that there are no harmful effects on your sinuses or nose, you will need regular check-ups with your doctor.
