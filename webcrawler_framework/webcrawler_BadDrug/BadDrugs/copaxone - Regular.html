Copaxone Treatment and Use

Copaxone | glatiramer injection is an immunomodulator made by the drug company Teva Neuroscience, Inc. that is used to treat the symptoms of relapse-remitting multiple sclerosis. This drug works by preventing the body from inflicting damage on its own nerve cells.

Copaxone comes in syringes that are pre-filled and are to be administered once a day subcutaneously, at the same time each day. Your doctor will give you your first dose of Copaxone, after which you will administer the medication yourself at home.

This medication can be injected into either the right and left arms, hips, thighs, or lower stomach. Since this medication mys be delivered into specific areas of these body parts, it is imperative that you read the manufacterer’s patient information sheet.
