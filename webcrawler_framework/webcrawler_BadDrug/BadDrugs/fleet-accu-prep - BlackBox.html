Fleet Accu-Prep Side Effects

Serious Side Effects of Fleet Accu-Prep

  • strong abdominal pain
  • persistent vomiting, a decrease in urination or extreme dizziness which may be signs of severe dehydration
  • blood in stool
  • serious allergic reaction with signs such as a rash, hives, difficulty breathing and swelling of the tongue, throat, mouth, or face

Common Side Effects

  • dehydration
  • diarrhea
  • upset stomach, cramps or nausea
  • bloating
  • headache
  • mild dizziness

Warnings & Recalls for Fleet Accu-Prep

Fleet Accu-Prep and Pregnancy

Accu-Prep and other laxatives are typically not recommended during pregnancy as it may remove certain nutrients from the body which are required for the unborn baby to grow. Consult your doctor before taking Fleet Accu-Prep if you are pregnant or breastfeeding. It is unknown if Accu-Prep transfers through breast-milk or may cause harm to a nursing baby.

General Warnings

Let your doctor know about your health and medical history to be sure that you may safely take Fleet Accu-Prep. Fleet Accu-Prep should not be taken by anyone with the following conditions:
  • allergies related to the ingredients of Fleet Accu-Prep or similar laxatives
  • heart failure
  • a history of a kidney disorder or if you are taking medications which may impair the kidneys
  • anyone who is elderly
Accu-Prep may increase the risk of developing kidney damage. Discuss this risks and benefits with your doctor before using Fleet Accu-Prep. Other medications may interact with Fleet Accu-Prep. Notify your healthcare provider about any other medicines you are currently taking to avoid potential health risks.

Fleet Accu-Prep Treatment and Use

Fleet Accu-Prep | momobasic sodium phosphate/dibasic sodium phosphates is an oral saline laxative product for bowel cleansing marketed by Laboratories Casen Fleet. Fleet Accu-Prep is prescribed to patients before certain such as a colonoscopy or radiography to remove stool from the bowels.

Fleet Accu-Prep should not be given to anyone under 5 years old.

How Does Fleet Accu-Prep Work?

Fleet Accu-Prep works be quickening the excretion of undigested foods in the intestines and colon. This accelerates bowel movements and “cleanses” the bowels.

Fleet Accu-Prep Dosage

Fleet Accu-Prep is available in an oral suspension. Take Accu-Prep only as prescribed by your doctor. Fleet Accu-Prep is generally taken in two doses before medical examinations depending on the patient. Dosage amounts include 2 to 3 pre-filled 15 mL units to be combined with 8 fluid ounces of clear liquid. Ask your doctor about which fluids may be taken with Accu-Prep. Your doctor may require that you begin a liquid diet and increase your intake of fluids to prevent dehydration.

Let your doctor about any questions you may have about Accu-Prep and other dietary information.
