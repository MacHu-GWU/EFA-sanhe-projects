Epogen Side Effects

Epogen | epoetin alfa has been know to cause side effects such as
  • headache
  • joint or muscle pain
  • nausea
  • vomiting
  • weight loss
  • mouth sores
  • somnolence
  • depression
  • redness, swelling, or pain at point of injection
Epogen carries the potential risk of a serious allergic reaction, which is characterized by
  • rash or hives
  • itching
  • swelling of the throat, face, tongue, lips, or eyes
  • difficulty breathing of swallowing
  • hoarseness
  • lack of energy
  • fainting
  • dizziness
If you experience any of these serious symptoms, seek medical treatment immediately. 

Warnings & Recalls for Epogen

  • Epogen may increase the risk for blood clots in the legs, lungs, or brain, and should not be used by those who have a history of heart disease or stroke, or patients who will be undergoing surgery soon.
  • Those who are treated with Epogen to reach normal hemoglobin levels might die sooner or develop serious heart conditions.
  • Tumors in cancer patients may accelerate in growth and result in a quicker death when taking Epogen.
  • Women who are pregnant or breastfeeding should not use Epogen.

Epogen Treatment and Use

Epogen | epoetin alfa is an injectable solution manufactured by Amgen for the treatment of anemia in patients who suffer from serious kidney disease, as well as those undergoing chemotherapy for non-myeloid cancers. By signalling the bone marrow to produce more red blood cells, Epogen works to treat and prevent anemia.

Epogen | epoetin alfa has been approved for the treatment of

  • Anemia in dialysis patients who suffer from chronic kidney disease
  • Low red blood cell count resulting from chemotherapy for the treatment of non-myeloid cancers
  • Anemia caused by zidovudine treatment for sufferers of HIV
  • Before and after surgery for anemic patients to decrease blood loss during the surgery, and as a result decrease the need for blood transfusion
