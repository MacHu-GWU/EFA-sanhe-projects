Fentanyl Side Effects

Fentanyl has been shown to cause common side effects, such as:
  • constipation
  • dizziness
  • drowsiness
  • dry mouth
  • headache
  • nausea
  • vomiting
  • weakness
  • tiredness
  • diarrhea
  • exaggerated sense of well-being
  • indigestion
  • loss of appetite
  • redness, swelling, or irritation at spot where patch was applied
  • insomnia
  • sweating
Fentanyl has also been shown to cause more serious side effects, such as:
  • blurred vision
  • fainting
  • hallucinations
  • muscle rigidity
  • seizures
  • severe dizziness
  • irregular heartbeat
  • irregular breathing patterns
  • trouble breathing
  • mood or mental changes
  • trouble urinating
  • chest pain
  • confusion
  • ulcers
  • bleeding
  • memory loss
  • flu-like symptoms
  • tremor
If you experience any serious symptoms, or if common side effects persist, contact your doctor and seek medical attention immediately.

Warnings & Recalls for Fentanyl

Fentanyl Transdermal System patches have been previously recalled due to a defect with the patch that caused this medication to be absorbed too quickly by the body. If you have used, or currently are using, a Fentanyl patch, and have suffered from any of the following, seek medical attention immediately:
  • excessive sedation
  • respiratory depression
  • slowed breathing
  • apnea
As a narcotic which is considered to be more potent than heroin, Fentanyl has a high risk for abuse. If you begin to become tolerant to the medication, or it seems to become less effective, talk with your doctor before self-adjusting your dosage.Do not stop taking Fentanyl suddenly, as this can result in symptoms of withdrawal. Fentanyl should never be combined with any other medications, as this can result in a fatal overdose.Patients who are taking Fentanyl should not drink alcohol or use other drugs while on this medication, as they can cause a severe, negative reaction.Fentanyl should not used by patients who are pregnant unless it is absolutely necessary. Fentanyl should not be used by patients who are breastfeeding, as this medication can be passed through breast milk.

Fentanyl Treatment and Use

Fentanyl is a generic opioid analgesic medication which is manufactured as a transdermal patch, a lozenge, and an injection. Fentanyl works to relieve pain and decrease the emotional response to pain by binding to opiate receptors in the brain and spinal cord. Dopamine levels are increased, resulting in intense, but temporary feelings of relief and euphoria.

Fentanyl is used in the treatment of the following conditions:

  • Chronic pain
  • Pain in patients with cancer who are using another opioid, such as morphine or oxycodone

Fentanyl should only be used for moderate to severe pain, and should not be used to treat mild or short-term pain, such as headaches or back pain.
