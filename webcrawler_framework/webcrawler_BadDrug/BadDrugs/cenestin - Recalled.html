Cenestin Side Effects

Serious Side Effects of Cenestin

  • Stop taking Cenestin and talk to your doctor immediately if you experience swelling of your stomach, eyes, face, mouth, arms, hands, legs, or feet.
  • Get medical attention if you develop hives, blisters, or a rash.
  • Talk to your doctor as soon as possible if you experience hoarseness, difficulty swallowing, or have trouble breathing.
  • If you have joint pain or your start to have movments that you cannot control, get medical help.
  • Signs of infection such as a fever, cough, and chills can be a sign of a seirous reaction to Cenestin. Talk to your doctor immeditately if you develop these symptms of an infection.

Common Side Effects of Cenestin

  • Cenestin may cause your stomach to become upset, heart burn, vomiting, constipation, and gas.
  • After taking Cenestin you may experience unexplained nervousness, depression, and dizziness.
  • You may have vaginal discharge, vaginal irritation, and your sexual desire may change during your Cenestin treatment.
  • Cenestin can cause unwanted growth of hair, loss of hair on your scalp, and darkening of the face.
  • Cenestin may cause you to experience unusual weight gain or weight loss, breast tenderness, cramps in your leg, and muscle tightness.

Warnings & Recalls for Cenestin

Cenestin Black Box Warnings

  • Taking estrogen may increase the risk of developing endometrial cancer or breast cancer.  When you are taking estrogen, your doctor may monitor you for any serious developments. Talk to your doctor if you experience persistent or abnormal vaginal bleeding.
  • Estrogen should never be used as a preventative medication for cardiovascular disease. or dementia. It can increase your risk of stroke, blood clots, heart attack and dementia.
  • The chances of developing a serious side effect may be dependent on dosage and length of treatment. Use the lowest amount of estrogen that is effective for you and for the shortest amount of time. To catch symptoms of adverse reactions, have regular physicals.

Cenestin Warnings

  • You should not take Cenestin if you have had a heart attack, blood clot in your lower body, stroke, a liver condition, abnormal vaginal bleeding, or a cancer that effects your hormones.
  • Cenestin can cause stunted growth and increased sexual development in children.
  • If you are older than 65, your risks of adverse reactions may be increased, talk to your doctor about other alternatives before you start taking Cenestin.
  • Tell your doctor bout all medications, herbal supplements  and vitamins you are currently taking or plan on taking during your Cenestin treatment.
  • Discuss all medical issues you have or have had with your doctor to ensure that you can take Cenestin safely.
  • Cenestin may harm unborn children. You should not used Cenestin if you are pregnant or plan on becoming pregnant.
  • Regularly examine yourself for any lumps that may have developed during your Cenestin treatment.
  • You should not smoke while taking Cenestin. It can increase your chances of developing blood clots, having a stroke, or having a heart attack.

Cenestin Treatment and Use

Cenestin | Synthetic Conjugated Estrogens come in tablets and is used as a treatment for patients with experiencing symptoms of menopause. These symptoms include hot flashes, and dryness, irritation, or burning sensation of the vagina. It is also sometimes used to prevent osteoporosis and to increase the amount of estrogen in those who have impaired estrogen production. In certain men and women, Cenestin can be used to treat prostate cancer and breast cancer.

How does Cenestin work?

The symptoms of menopause are caused be the body’s decreased estrogen production. Cenestin contains a mixture of nine different synthetic estrogens that can fulfill the work of the lacking estrogen in the body, fighting the effects of reduced estrogen production.

Cenestin Dosage Information

A patient’s Cenestin dosage is dependent on the condition to be treated and is adjusted based  on response to the treatment. Your doctor may prescribe Cenestin to you in a cyclic dosage, during which you will take Cenestin for three weeks, then go without a dose for one week.

Typically, Cenestin is taken once daily. Take Cenestin before or after a meal to prevent your stomach from becoming upset. You may also have to increase your calcium and vitamin D intake while taking Cenestin. Discuss consuming grapefruit or grapefruit products with your doctor.

During your treatment, your doctor may require you to have regular visits and medical testing done to monitor your condition for any adverse developments.
