Lyrica Side Effects

Lyrica | pregabalin side effects and adverse reactions include the following:
  • Muscle pain and spasms
  • Sinusitis
  • “Foggy” feeling
  • General malaise
  • Euphoria
  • Gas or diarrhea
  • Fatigue
  • Constipation
  • Psychomotor disruptions
  • Twitches
  • Chest pain
  • Tremor
  • Changes in appetite
  • Joint ache
  • Weight gain
  • Drowsiness
  • Allergic reaction
  • Swelling of the hands and feet

Warnings & Recalls for Lyrica

  • Lyrica can cause a severe allergic reaction in very rare instances. If you exhibit the symptoms of severe allergic reaction, seek medical help immediately.
  • If you take angiotensin converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitors or diabetes medications, tall your doctor. Together with Lyrica, these drugs give you have a higher chance of swelling or getting hives. Swelling increases the risk of congestive heart failure.
  • Do not drink alcohol if you take Lyrica.
  • Tell your doctor if you take any other medications for mood, emotional, or psychiatric disorders as well as for pain. These may adversely interact with Lyrica.
  • Do not stop taking this medication unless your doctor tells you to. This can increase your risk of seizure.
  • Lyrica may cause a muscle problem called rhabdomyolysis. Tell your doctor if you experience muscle pain, weakness or tenderness.

Lyrica Treatment and Use

The pain medication Lyrica | pregabalin is an anticonvulsant drug manufactured by the pharmaceutical company Pfizer. Lyrica was the first fibromyalgia treatment approved by the FDA and is indicated for the following conditions:

  • Fibromyalgia
  • Diabetic nerve pain
  • Pain after shingles
  • Partial onset seizures in adults with Epilepsy
  • Generalized anxiety disorder or GAD  (in the European Union)
  • Alcohol withdrawl symptoms

For treatment of GAD, Lyrica was shown to be similar to the drugs lorazepam, Xanax | alprazolam, and Effexor | venlafaxine. Though Lyrica is best known as a pain medication, its therapeutic use for anxiety has been shown to be effective with a lower risk of the “fog” that comes with benzodiazepines such as the aforementioned drugs.

How does Lyrica work?

The exact mode of action for Lyrica to treat neuropathic pain disorders and anxiety as well as other mood disorders is not completely known. It is believed that Lyrica | pregablin works by calming the overly stimulated nerves that cause chronic pain disorders such as fibromyalgia. It is this same believed mechanism that reduces the symptoms of general anxiety disorder and alcohol withdrawal in individuals.

Lyrica dosing

Lyrica starts off at 150 mg for all conditions that it treats. It either comes in tablet or oral solution form. The doses are divided in two or three daily incriments and daily maximum dosage ranges from:

  • Ddiabetic peripheral neuropathy pain (DPN): 300 mg maximum
  • Post herpetic neuralgia (PHN): 600 mg maximum
  • Adjunctive partial onset seizure therapy in adults: 600 mg maximum
  • Fibromyalgia: 450 mg maximum
