Pulmonary Hypertension	
		
Causes Incidence + Risk Factors	
		
l--- lifestyle - smoking, alcohol, diet (salt, fat intake), exercise			
l--- genetic predisposition			
l--- past use of fen-phen (dexfenfluramine and phentermine)			
l--- chronic liver disease (cirrhosis)			
l--- rheumatic disorders, such as scleroderma or lupus			
l--- lung conditions, such as tumors, blood clots (pulmonary emboli), emphysema, COPD, pulmonary fibrosis			
l--- heart diseases, such as aortic valve disease, left heart failure, mitral valve disease, congenital heart disease			
l--- blood clot in large pulmonary artery (thromboembolic disease)			
l--- high altitude living (higher than 8,000 ft/2,439 m)			
l--- climbing to or hiking to altitudes higher than 8,000 ft (2,439 m) without acclimating first			
l--- obesity			
l--- sleep apnea and other sleep disorders			
l--- sickle cell anemia			
l--- use of certain stimulant drugs, such as cocaine			
l--- AIDS	
		
Symptoms			
l--- shortness of breath (dyspnea) with everyday activities			
l--- fatigue			
l--- dizziness or fainting spells (syncope)			
l--- swelling (edema) in ankles, legs, abdomen			
l--- chest pressure or pain			
l--- bluish color to lips and skin (cyanosis)			
l--- racing pulse or heart palpitations	
		
Signs + Test			
l--- blood test			
l--- doppler echocardiogram			
l--- chest x-ray			
l--- 6 minute walk test			
l--- spirometer			
l--- polysomnigram or overnight oximetry			
l--- right heart catheterization			
l--- ventilation perfusion scan (V/Q scan)			
l--- pulmonary angiogram			
l--- open-lung biopsy			
l--- transesophageal echocardiogram			
l--- MRI			
l--- chest CT scan
			
Treatment			
l--- no cure			
l--- stop smoking			
l--- medication			
l--- surgical treatment to remove blood clot (pulmonary thromboendarterctomy) or open-heart surgery (atrial septostomy)			
l--- lung or heart-lung transplantation
			
Treatment Motions			
l--- reduce stress			
l--- rest			
l--- keep active			
l--- avoid smokers			
l--- avoid pregnancy or birth control pills 			
l--- healthy diet - reduce salt intake (1,500 - 2,400 mg/day)			
l--- avoid sitting in hot tub or sauna, long hot showers/baths			
l--- avoid lifting heavy objects or weights
			
Complications			
l--- right-sided heart failure (cor pumonale)			
l--- blood clots			
l--- irregular heartbeat (arrhthymia)			
l--- coughing up blood (hemoptysis)
			
Blood Pressure	
                               Classification		
l--- Systolic/Diastolic (mmGh)			
       l---  <120 / <80	optimal		
       l---   <130 / <85	normal		
       l---   130-139 / 85-89	high-normal		
       l---    140-159 / 90-99	grade 1 hypertension (mild)		
       l---    160-179 / 100-109	grade 2 hypertension (moderate)		
       l---    =180 / =110	grade 3 hypertension (severe)		
       l---    140-159 / <90	isolated systolic hypertension (grade 1)		
       l---    =160 / <90	isolated systolic hypertension (grade 2)
		
*NOTE: if hypertensive, take 3 readings and drop first. Record next 2 and take average.
